# Pacifist — Strategy Primer

The Pacifist trades military leverage for Psionic leverage. Both act objectives and the Act 3 grand ambitions run through Psionics — spending them while Empath is declared, and ending Act 3 with fewer than your rivals collectively hold. Your kit gives you the deepest Psionic access in the game (Witnesses to declare Empath, Well of Empathy to store unlimited Psionics) but imposes a hard constraint: trophies and captives cost you objective progress. You are not a pacifist in the sense of being passive; you are a pacifist in the sense that violence is expensive and you will find ways to get what you want without it.

---

## Act 2 — Empathy at Scale *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two tracks feed your objective marker. **Commit to Pacifism** gives 3 free advancements per chapter if you end it with no trophies or captives — treat this as your floor, not your ceiling. **Spread Empathy** advances the marker by 1 each time you spend a Psionic during prelude while Empath is declared. Declare Empath early and often; spending Psionics on prelude actions doubles as objective progress. In a 4-player game you need roughly 3 Spread Empathy advancements per chapter on top of Commit to Pacifism; plan your Psionic throughput accordingly.

- Get Witnesses to Psionic planets early — they declare Empath and produce Psionics simultaneously. Flip one at a time to keep declarations available every chapter.
- On Administration turns, tax a Psionic city to gain a Psionic, then spend it during prelude to advance your objective — this is a near-free objective tick.
- Court cards are obtained via the Relic/Psionic prelude combo, not influence fights: influencing with a Psionic and securing with a Relic lets you grab uncontested cards in a single turn without competing.
- If you have a captive or trophy, trade it away at the Summit. Budget a payment — rivals know you want it gone more than they want it.
- Staying Regent is strongly preferable: Truce Law shields you, Imperial ships soak Blight hits, and you can move Witnesses into new systems without building loyal escorts. Avoid First Regent — Imperial Demand will be your problem, not your tool.
- You can still battle without taking trophies: skirmish dice damage enemy ships and Blight without destroying them, disrupting control without adding to your burden.

**Key kit:**
- **Well of Empathy** — stores unlimited Psionics regardless of resource slot count; gives you an overwhelming Empath advantage and deters raiding (each raid die averages one self-hit per key, so attackers bleed ships to steal Psionics).
- **Witnesses** — primary mechanism for declaring Empath; secondary Psionic source. Their value is the declaration, not the resource. Don't flip all at once.
- **Relic + Psionic combo** — your court acquisition engine. Influence with a Psionic prelude action; secure with a Relic prelude action. Keeps you out of agent wars that would end in captives.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Magnate** — great matchup. Psionic Cartel (Magnate card) is easy for you to grab and guarantees Empath wins regardless of how many Psionics you spend. If Magnate takes it, they must discard it eventually, making it easy for you to reclaim.
- **Founder** — the Commonwealth helps you: free city actions give resource access without taxing rivals, and Armistices limit the bullying you're exposed to. If Commonwealth ambition is Empath, you get Psionics and the ambition power together.
- **Believer** — watch the Doctrine. If Empath carries an Ideal, you can only declare it when Doctrine aligns. Keep Empath Ideal-free when possible; otherwise Believer controls your scoring turn.
- **Blight Speaker** — difficult. You cannot destroy Blight. Keep it damaged with skirmish-only battles; negotiate at Summits for rivals to take your Blight trophies. If Blight Speaker succeeds Act 2, strongly consider pivoting in Act 3.
- **Planet Breaker** — Psionic outrage is painful (not game-ending — you can still spend Psionics during prelude while outraged). Prioritise Heartworld Refugees if a Psionic planet is destroyed; it floods you with Psionics and clears Outrage. Negotiate pre-emptively for rivals to take any Planet Breaker trophies you might earn defending planets.

## Act 3 — The Empathy Heist *(Complexity 4/5, Difficulty 5/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two grand ambitions, both always active. The first — **no trophies or captives** — is familiar; treat it as even more critical here, since missing it in chapters 3 or 4 is potentially game-ending. The second — **rivals collectively hold more Psionics than you** — is the hardest grand ambition in the game to guarantee. Do not build your primary scoring plan around it. Your real engine is **winning Empath every chapter**: it scores points and denies them from rivals simultaneously. A rival holding Psionics to score Empath is also a rival helping your second grand ambition.

- Front-load Empath wins in chapters 1–2 while the ambition is worth fewer points; by chapters 3–4 the value of Empath versus your second grand ambition is comparable — at that point choose based on table state.
- To attempt the second grand ambition in a late chapter: dump your own Psionics and force Psionics into rivals' slots in the last round. Use **Reconcile with the Outraged** (Call to Order; needs an outraged rival) or **Rebuke of the Empaths** (Edicts; needs at least one rival without Psionic outrage and a free resource slot). Both require controlling the last round — prioritise securing the council or holding initiative cards.
- "C" Fate players are your allies for the second grand ambition: they don't compete on Power, have less incentive to dump Psionics, and may simply hold them.
- If you're far behind and the second grand ambition isn't achievable, consider Portal Seekers as a substitute Power source — but it requires defending the card, which is a challenge without violence.

**Key kit:**
- **Empathy For All (Law)** — gives outrage to anyone who causes harm in a system where they already hold the matching resource. Annoys rivals; generates the outrage you need for Reconcile with the Outraged.
- **Reconcile with the Outraged (Summit action)** — give an outraged rival a Psionic (from your supply), gain a Favor, remove their outrage. On the last round of a chapter: spend Psionics here to simultaneously dump yours and add to a rival's tally. Also useful throughout to harvest Favors from Empathy For All triggers.
- **Rebuke of the Empaths (Edict)** — gives a Psionic to every player without Psionic outrage. Use via Edicts on the last round if rivals have non-Psionic outrage (or none). Requires the event die to cooperate, or securing the council to guarantee Edicts.
- **Cards that let you declare Empath** (Galactic Bards, Tycoon's Ambition, Psionic Liaison, Faithful cards, etc.) — Witnesses are gone in Act 3. Stockpile one-use Empath-declaring cards during Act 2; they become your primary mechanism for declaration.
- **Cards that secure initiative or the council** (Lattice Spies, Song of Freedom, Council Insider, Unions) — the Psionic heist must land on the last round of the chapter. Controlling initiative or the council is how you guarantee that window.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Magnate** — complicated. If you hold Psionic Cartel, you win Empath but cannot achieve the second grand ambition. If Magnate holds it, they'll dump it on the last round, blocking you. Ideal: keep Cartel away from Magnate all game; consider letting them have it only in chapter 4 if the math works in your favour.
- **Believer** — worst matchup. A Doctrine kept out of alignment with Empath's Ideal locks you out of declarations entirely (unless you destroy a Believer city — which you'd obviously rather not). Keep Empath Ideal-free; if it gains an Ideal, the Doctrine is now a weapon against you.
- **Overlord** — excellent matchup. Reconcile with the Outraged almost directly counters their objective: spend Psionics to remove their outrage as fast as they generate it, gaining Favors in the process.
- **Naturalist** — nuanced. They won't dump Psionics willingly (they need them), which helps your second grand ambition. They also won't fight you for Power. But unchecked Blight is your problem without violence — Empathy For All at least taxes anyone battling Blight in systems where they hold matching resources.
- **Peacekeeper** — bad. You can't reliably end ceasefires, so you depend on the 3rd and 4th player to do it. Not a crisis, but you're at their mercy.
- **Judge** — risky for the second grand ambition. Judges can reallocate resources before scoring; a tie they engineer can flip your ambition. If Judge is present, lean more heavily on winning Empath instead.
- **Planet Breaker** — still bad, now for different reasons. Psionic outrage on the whole table can sometimes help (outraged rivals may keep Psionics in slots for Rebuke), but your inability to stop Planet Breaker's grand ambitions without violence is a structural problem.

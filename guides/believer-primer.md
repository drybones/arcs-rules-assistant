# Believer — Strategy Primer

The Believer wins by flooding the action deck with Faithful cards — versatile, publicly visible action cards that everyone wants but only the Believer can fully exploit. Act 1 is a gruelling bootstrapping problem: you need to win ambitions to advance your objective, but you have almost no tools to help you win them, and the Teach actions you need to build those tools cost you the same precious action economy. Survive Act 1 and the snowball inverts: Act 2 gives you The Young Light, letting you curate your hand and flip the Doctrine at will, while Act 3 turns ambition declaration itself into your scoring engine. The Believer rewards patient fundamentals and punishes recklessness — an early mistake compounds all campaign.

---

## Act 1 — Lay the Foundations of Faith *(Complexity 4/5, Difficulty 5/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance by winning ambitions — you score once per pip on the top Faithful card in your stack, so more Teaches means larger advances per ambition win. Aim to Teach at least twice in Chapter 1 (zero risk of scrapping a Faithful card), then recalculate each chapter how many Teaches you need to reach the next pip threshold versus how many ambitions you can reliably win. Prioritise the ambition your rivals are least positioned to contest; Keeper is a natural early target if you have a Relic city, given the Relic icon on Faithful Disciples.

- At setup, confirm at least one Guild card in the Court matches a Loyal city type — Teaching without a matching city is impossible. Consider a double-city start to guarantee early Teaches, accepting reduced mobility.
- Teach when you can guarantee securing the resulting Faithful card yourself: last in turn order, or with 2 Psionics + 1 Relic in hand to Teach, influence, and secure in one pass. Teaching while holding initiative is the worst time — you are handing rivals a free Faithful card.
- Balance city variety across resource types (there are only 2 Guild cards per type in the Court deck); check which types have already left the deck before committing a city slot.
- Spread cities of different types rather than doubling up — you have only 5 cities and need a broad base to Teach on whatever Guild cards appear.

**Key kit:**
- **Faithful Disciples** — your only starting Guild card; situationally useful for the extra action pip, but the Relic icon is its main early-game value. Grows stronger as Faithful cards become more common.
- **Faithful Action Cards** — the foundation of everything. Their unique backs make them public knowledge in rivals' hands; use this to lead cards they can't surpass. Every copy a rival secures is one copy that isn't yours — don't let one player accumulate several.
- **Gate Stations (Lore)** — a city on a gate covers up to three resource types, dramatically expanding your Teach options. Prioritise securing this.
- **Gatekeepers (Guild)** — prelude places a Loyal ship in every gate; effectively one move from building in any cluster. Especially useful with a double-city start and no catapult.
- **Mining/Shipping Interest (Guild)** — prelude shores up Tycoon while generating the Material/Fuel you need for new cities.
- **Silver-Tongues (Guild)** — do not let rivals carry this into Act 2. Your Act 1 success reward (The Young Light) arrives unprotected and is an immediate Silver-Tongues target. Secure it yourself or exhaust it this Act.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — directly competes for ambitions and hoovers up Court cards via Dealmakers, threatening your Faithful cards. Negotiate: multiple agents on a card you need signals you want it, incentivising the Steward to Bargain off it rather than contest. If their Edicts are draining you, seize with an event card and call a Summit to exit the Empire.
- **Partisan** — Informants/Spy swaps the highest-value action card from whoever holds it; your first few Faithful cards (added at value 9) are their easiest targets. Use Faithful cards immediately rather than sitting on them.
- **Advocate** — Guild Investigators can block Teach slots and scoop your Faithful cards. Convince them to Recover on cards ineligible for Teach. Disrupt their ambition positioning to nullify Guild Overseers and slow their card flow.
- **Magnate** — a natural ally: trade them a city that's no longer useful for Teaching in exchange for resources you need to win ambitions. Be proactive — outbid rivals for their Merchant League deals.

## Act 2 — The Young Light Rises *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance by winning ambitions that have Ideal markers. Ideal markers are placed by declaring with a Faithful card. You need power totalling 18 (3-player) or 16 (4-player) from those wins — roughly winning the top-scoring ambition each chapter. Bonus city power (5 extra per ambition won) is your most efficient lever: if you enter Act 2 close to unlocking it, go build those final cities early. Ensure every ambition you win already has an Ideal marker; if a rival declares one without, use a Faithful card to declare it again and place the marker — but weigh the power you're handing them against your objective need.

- Once your objective is secure, spend spare actions placing Ideal markers on all 5 ambitions. Enter Act 3 with a fully marked board if possible.
- Bonus city power makes even low-scoring ambitions worth winning; you no longer need to fight rivals for the juiciest marker every chapter.
- The Doctrine starts on Wisdom unless you led the power race into Act 2 (then Zeal). Plan around this: Zeal = move/battle/tax/influence (aggressive, flexible); Wisdom = build/secure (situational, slow). Hold The Young Light to flip the Doctrine via Set Doctrine when Edicts fire.

**Key kit:**
- **The Young Light** — the centrepiece. Its prelude lets you swap a non-event card for any Faithful card played this round, once per turn: use it to hold initiative by leading Faithful cards rivals can't surpass, or to surpass when rivals lead them. Protect it obsessively.
- **Set Doctrine (Edict)** — changes which side Faithful cards must be played on and which ambitions can be declared with them. Flip to match the Ideal markers you want to declare, or flip to strand rivals with a hand full of useless-suited Faithful cards.
- **Doctrinal Faith (Law)** — every Faithful-declared ambition gets an Ideal marker. Rivals are often forced to declare with Faithful cards to hit their own objectives, putting markers on the board for you.
- **Galactic Bards** — if available (Partisan Act 1 result), acquire immediately. Pivoting with a Faithful card while still placing an Ideal marker is exceptionally flexible for objective completion.
- **Guild Struggle / Keeper's Solidarity (Lore)** — Guild Struggle can retrieve The Young Light if it's been discarded; Keeper's Solidarity (requires Psionics) forces rivals to roll 6 keys to steal it.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Planet Breaker** — will force Psionic Outrage, potentially discarding The Young Light. Kneecap them before they complete their Act 2 objective; they become a catastrophic problem in Act 3 if left unchecked.
- **Blight Speaker / Hegemon** — both can win ambitions toward their Act 2 objectives late in the act, threatening to swipe the ambitions you need. Watch their progress markers as the chapter count winds down.
- **Founder** — if the Commonwealth ambition has an Ideal marker, staying in and helping win it is efficient; leaving and winning the same ambition yourself nets massive power but draws everyone's ire. Choose based on your power position.
- **Advocate** — can spend accumulated favors (3) to take The Young Light directly. Avoid holding Guild cards of suits the Advocate doesn't possess, to reduce the magnetic attraction of your tableau to their demand.

## Act 3 — Checkmate, Atheists *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 1/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score grand ambitions by having Ideal markers on declared ambitions — first grand ambition at 2 declared ambitions with Ideals, both at 3. Point values for grand ambitions scale sharply in Chapters 3–4, so play the long game: early chapters, declare ambitions that lack Ideal markers (even ones you won't win) to cover the board. Once all 5 Ideal markers are down, every ambition declaration — including rivals' — feeds your scoring. Create a zugzwang: if rivals don't declare, they can't close the power gap; if they do, they score your grand ambitions for you.

- Prioritise placing Ideal markers before rivals can use the final ambition marker to declare a non-Ideal ambition and block your chapter scoring.
- Hold initiative via The Prophet (same prelude as The Young Light) to control which ambitions get declared and place markers on uncovered ambitions before rivals act.
- Dominant Faith now bars non-Faithful declaration of Ideal ambitions — use Set Doctrine to lock rivals off a specific ambition they're farming, or keep Doctrine matching the majority of Ideal markers to make all markers simultaneously declarable.
- **Smol Believer:** if you have no cities on the board, Breaking the Faith cannot be triggered. In Chapters 3–4, deliberately avoid rebuilding cities; the grand ambition scoring alone can carry you. If you must rebuild, place cities matching rivals' best Guild card types — destroying them triggers their Outrage, not just yours.
- Watch for Populist Demands in the Court — its Crisis is the only effect in the game that can remove a marker from a *declared* ambition. If all 3 markers are already placed on Ideals before it resolves, your chapter scoring is safe.

**Key kit:**
- **The Prophet** — The Young Light upgraded (theft-proof, same prelude). With Faithful cards plentiful by Act 3, you should be surpassing or seizing nearly every round. Losing it to Outrage freezes the Doctrine permanently on its current side; mitigate by using the prelude to move it off your tableau before a rival acts.
- **Dominant Faith (Law)** — ambitions with Ideal markers can only be declared with Faithful cards. Anyone who needs to declare must use your cards, on your Doctrine's terms. Combined with Set Doctrine, you control the menu.
- **Breaking the Faith (Law)** — rivals who destroy your Loyal cities may remove an Ideal marker from an undeclared ambition. Time your Ideal placement to have all 3 markers down before rivals can react; an undeclared ambition with no marker is only vulnerable if the ambition marker hasn't been placed yet.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Planet Breaker** — destroys planets, triggering Breaking the Faith, advancing their own grand ambitions, and potentially discarding The Prophet all in one hit. Rally rivals against them and target their ships and upgrades relentlessly.
- **Naturalist / Guardian** — each adds an extra ambition (Blightkin / Edenguard), giving rivals a permanent non-Ideal declaration outlet. If you have a spare Ideal marker, place it on these new ambitions immediately; rivals stopping these C-Fates will then do so using your card types.
- **Advocate** — Guild Loyalty lets them Provoke Outrage without losing Guild cards, making them a likely Breaking the Faith trigger. They retain Advocate's Demand too; stay alert to both threats simultaneously.
- **Conspirator** — must obey Dominant Faith and the Doctrine when placing Conspiracies. Their supply of disposable Guild cards makes them dangerous. Declaring Conspiracy ambitions is safer than foiling (a wrong guess loses them progress); be prepared to flip the Doctrine to access those ambitions if needed. Do not let them control the Doctrine.
- **Overlord** — incentivised to Provoke Outrage for their objective; may incidentally trigger Breaking the Faith. Anticipate their pathing and contest planets they need before they arrive.

# Survivalist — Strategy Primer

The Survivalist enters in Act 3 as a C Fate with a Final Objective that wins outright at chapter end. Their identity is passive accumulation: build Bunkers across the map, cohabit with Blight, and let crises and ambition wins stack advancements without bringing you into direct conflict. The flexible two-track objective — Blight crises on Bunker planets, or ambition wins scaled to Bunkers and Captives — makes them hard to fully shut down, but the whole engine depends on Bunkers staying on the board. Every Bunker costs one of your own agents to build, so your 10-agent supply is also your scoring ceiling.

---

## Act 3 — Hunker Down *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 18 times (3p) or 14 times (4p) via two parallel tracks. **Protect Against the Blight**: build Bunkers on planets with Blight; each crisis at those planets advances once per Blight-bearing Bunker. **Keep the People Safe**: win any ambition to advance once per Captive or Bunker, whichever is lower. Both tracks share the Bunker requirement — building them is the first and most important priority each chapter.

- Lead with **Protect Against the Blight** when Blight is plentiful: it bypasses the ambition system and is harder for rivals to stop than beating you in an ambition race.
- Secure the council to trigger crises on demand mid-round rather than waiting for random Event card rolls; with 3–4 Bunkers under Blight, a single council-called crisis can cover 20–25% of your total requirement.
- Fall back on **Keep the People Safe** when Blight is sparse or crisis rolls run cold; stockpile resources for a specific ambition rather than pivoting every chapter, since you only need to win once or twice per act, not every chapter.
- Tyrant is near-forbidden by Vow of Survival (you score only 2nd-place points), but the **advancements** from winning it still count — if a rival declares Tyrant for their own reasons, winning it should become a primary focus.
- Balance Bunker count against Captive count for Keep the People Safe: six Captives with one Bunker still nets only one advancement. Build and capture in tandem.
- Track your burst potential each chapter — a combined Blight-crisis-plus-ambition-win burst tops out at roughly 6–10 advancements, so you need quieter advances earlier before attempting the finishing push.
- Protect the Blight itself. Unlike Blight Speaker or Naturalist, you cannot create new Blight; if rivals strip it from the board your entire passive track collapses into an ambition-only game that everyone can coordinate to block.

**Key kit:**
- **Building Bunkers** — the foundation of both objective tracks; each build also captures 1 rival agent, growing your Keep the People Safe ceiling. Bunkers are Tough (2 hits to damage) and cannot be raided, but every one costs an agent from your supply — treat lost Bunkers as permanent losses.
- **Vow of Survival** — keeps captives after Tyrant is scored up to your Bunker count; without it, each chapter-end wipes your Keep the People Safe potential. Also bars you from declaring Tyrant, so prioritise Blight-track scoring to reduce ambition dependency.
- **Captive Release** — the edict your kit introduces; rivals who control a Bunker and have more Power can demand you return their captives or trigger outrage of that planet type. As a C Fate you will frequently have less Power than A/B rivals, so controlling your own Bunkers is non-negotiable. When forced to choose, weigh captive loss against how crippling the specific outrage would be.
- **Ship-granting court cards** (Prison Wardens, Skirmishers, Court Enforcers, Gate Keepers, Loyal Marines, Sycophants) — Blight crises and Bunker defence bleed ships constantly; Prelude effects providing 3 ships in a system or 1 per gate replace losses and deter attacks.
- **Material/Fuel court cards** (Loyal Engineers, Mining Interest, Elder Broker, Material Liaisons, Loyal Pilots, Shipping Interest, Fuel Liaisons) — Material funds early Bunker builds and ship replacement; Fuel repositions your fleet to cover new Blight sites as the act progresses.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Naturalist** — can move Blight off your Bunker planets or suppress Blight crises in their controlled systems, attacking both objective tracks at once. They also control Blighted systems for their own objective, positioning them to use Captive Release and kill Bunkers. Counter by contesting Blightkin ambition — winning it is a nightmare scenario for the Naturalist and one of the few ambitions you are naturally well-placed to compete for.
- **Blight Speaker** — shared interest in triggering crises creates an uneasy alliance, but Blight Fury lets them move Blight off your planets, and your cohabitating pieces eat hits first when crises fire. Negotiate a mutual-crisis truce if possible; failing that, prioritise ship production to absorb the punishment.
- **Founder** — Commonwealth Free buildings are useful and Armistice can protect Bunkers, but Political Intrigue threatens your agent margin and Transfer Asset can strip captives. Secure Political Intrigue immediately if it appears in the court before it fires.
- **Warden** — Feudal Law deters rivals from entering your Bunker sectors if you are within a Fief; Harm between Lords and Vassals triggers multi-city-type outrage. Exploit unclaimed sectors to establish your own Fief around your key Bunkers.
- **Pacifist** — Empathy for All makes harming Bunkers provoke outrage of that planet type, which rivals do not normally incur. This is a significant passive deterrent; rivals may decide the guild-card and resource loss is not worth attacking you.
- **Peacekeeper** — Ceasefire requires attackers to spend a Weapon to harm you; Arsenal Keepers limits Weapon availability. If rivals start to coordinate against you, spread Bunkers across multiple clusters to multiply the Weapons required to police all of them.
- **Planet Breaker** — Breaking Worlds harms Bunkers (they are harmed like buildings), but broken planets still accept Bunkers and you are broadly less threatened than most fates. Their presence draws table attention away from you — typically a net positive.
- **Hegemon** (if Act 2 success) — Mind Managers becomes exceptional with your kit: build a Bunker, take the target's agent as captive, and use Influence to loop Mind Managers continuously. Vow of Survival keeps those captives. Pursue this combination aggressively if Hegemon succeeded.
- **Caretaker** — their extra Event card in the deck is extra crisis rolls; Harvester and Seeker golems provide Material income and free catapult moves for placing remote Bunkers. A strong pivot target or friendly ally.
- **Believer** — Doctrine of Wisdom reduces aggressive actions available to rivals and unlocks build actions; Young Light can recur Wisdom cards. A reliable friendly presence when Blight is thin and you need to build freely.

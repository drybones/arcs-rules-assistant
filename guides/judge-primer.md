# Judge — Strategy Primer

The Judge enters the Reach in Act 3 with a single, deceptively precise mandate: force ties. Where every other fate competes to *win* ambitions, the Judge competes to make them *equal* — showing up just ahead, then redistributing resources at the last moment to drag a rival up alongside. The core tool is *The Arbiter*, a protected Guild card that lets you give resources, Trophies, and Captives to any rival at scoring. Without it, the objective is nearly unachievable. The Judge rewards players who track every card suit in play, manage Summit timing obsessively, and treat the action deck as a ledger as much as a hand of cards.

---

## Act 3 — The Gavel Falls *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 5/5)*

**Objective focus:** Force six ties for first place on scored ambitions across Act 3's four chapters. Ties must happen at chapter end — so the whole act is preparation for that moment. Aim for at least three ties in the first two chapters; falling behind this pace leaves almost no room for error. Warlord and Tyrant are the highest-priority targets: their counts reset every chapter (Trophies and Captives are lost at scoring), unlimited quantities can be given via *The Arbiter* without hitting resource slot limits, and one returned Loyal piece per transfer can help thin awkward numbers.

- The strongest position is to be *winning* an ambition, then use *The Arbiter* to close the gap — giving away as little as possible (one to three components to cover a lead of one to three; four or more requires two to close). Trailing by more than three on a resource ambition is nearly impossible to tie from unless the leader holds the surplus via suited Guild cards.
- Pace resource acquisition like a taxation engine: secure Guild cards broadly regardless of suit, and keep resource slots filled across all five types. Maximum flexibility at scoring requires coverage of whatever ambitions are declared each chapter.
- End each chapter with initiative if possible — you want to declare ambitions early next chapter before rivals can establish an untieable lead.
- Go Outlaw if the Imperial Trust is filling: frozen resources cannot be given through *The Arbiter*, and if you are inside the Empire, the Imperial Demand can strip the very resources you planned to redistribute.

**Key kit:**
- ***The Arbiter*** — the entire objective runs through this card. Protect it from Relic Outrage at all costs; losing it reduces your tie-forcing ability to near zero. Never let Relic planets be destroyed (see Planet Breaker matchup).
- ***Vow of Fairness*** — makes your Captives and Trophies raidable at a one-key cost. A meaningful downside since Tyrant and Warlord are your primary targets; keep your board defensible against opportunistic raiders.
- ***Judge's Chosen*** title — assigned to a rival each time *Judge Sets Agenda* fires. That rival can move and battle with your ships. Give it to the rival who can do least damage: someone low on Aggression cards, Fuel, and Weapons, or who cannot afford to trigger the Outrage type of your cities.
- ***Judge Sets Agenda*** Edict — places any unplaced ambition marker on any ambition, at the cost of losing Power and handing *Judge's Chosen* to a rival. Use it sparingly until forced; once triggered once, the downside is unavoidable, so use it freely thereafter. Pairs well with seizing the Imperial Council to fire Edicts on your terms.
- **Imperial Council** — control it to suppress Summits. Summits are the primary threat to your objective: rivals can trade resources freely during Negotiations, dismantling carefully constructed tie positions. Hold the Council decided; only open it when you need to trigger *Judge Sets Agenda* yourself.
- **Administration and Aggression tracking** — know how many of each suit remain unplayed each chapter and who holds them. This tells you how much rival capacity exists to tax resources (breaking ties) or raid yours (disrupting *The Arbiter*).

**Notable matchups:**
- **Believer (Act 2 success)** — Faithful cards flood the action deck, suppressing Event cards (good for you) but adding more Administration access and locking ambitions via Ideal Markers (bad). Destroy their cities to dislodge Ideal Markers and free locked ambitions. A hard uphill fight.
- **Conspirator** — they advance when a tie involves the player they guessed, and they will guess you on every ambition. Expect to need two others to tie in your place, forcing you to give more away. Declare ambitions as early as possible; lean into *Judge Sets Agenda* and *Judge's Chosen* to disrupt their conspiracy placements.
- **Pacifist** — will hammer Empath repeatedly and have a Power-race advantage. One of their Grand Ambitions can be blocked by giving them a Captive or Trophy via *The Arbiter*; balance that against losing a tie opportunity.
- **Planet Breaker** — Relic Outrage destroys *The Arbiter*. This is a hard stop. Negotiate first; if that fails, redirect your fleet (or *Judge's Chosen*) to destroy their ships before a Relic planet is cracked.
- **Magnate** — Monopolies and Cartels lock resources into unshareable banks you cannot touch with *The Arbiter*. If they have succeeded Act 2, consider pivoting to a Power race rather than objective completion.
- **Believer / Magnate (general)** — both can restructure the resource economy so fundamentally that *The Arbiter* loses much of its leverage. Identify this early and adjust your win condition accordingly.
- **Caretaker** — their Act success adds an Event card, increasing Summit frequency. Also watch for *Unstable Harvester*: if triggered late in a chapter after you have acted, it can erase a full chapter of tie setup. Secure it before the Crisis fires.
- **Naturalist / Guardian** — each adds a sixth ambition (*Blightkin* / *Edenguard*) that *The Arbiter* cannot tie. Focus on the base five; only contest their ambition to stop an outright win if necessary. If both are present, consider racing Power instead.
- **Advocate** — Guild card prelude discards change scoring counts at the last moment, similar to a late Summit. Their double-counting of Guild icons makes resource ambitions very difficult to tie against them. Concentrate on Warlord and Tyrant where counts stay small.

# Hegemon — Strategy Primer

The Hegemon arrives in Act 2 as a Flagship fate with a single driving logic: plant Banners on empty planets, then protect them. Banners are not just objective tokens — through Song of the Banner they function as both a City and a Starport, and they form a teleportation network that gives the Hegemon an action economy no other fate can match. The flip side is a reduced fleet (four ships are scrapped at setup) and complete dependence on clearing planets before placing — Free buildings, Rival ships, and Imperial ships all block placement. Play is a careful balance between aggression to clear space and restraint to avoid painting a target on yourself too early.

---

## Act 2 — Plant the Banners *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 14 times by scoring 1 per Banner on the map at each chapter end, then multiplied again when you win ambitions. The sweet spot is 4–5 Banners at chapter end. Get Banners down fast — the network compounds both your action economy and your objective score — then shift toward winning ambitions to close out the 14.

- Place your Flagship at setup near Material/Psionic planets and the cluster 2/3 or 5/6 gap zones; these let you chain Slipstream hops to plant multiple Banners in a single Prelude.
- Three Banners on diverse resource types is the minimum safe baseline; aim for more to absorb inevitable losses.
- Use Song of the Banner's Call Spirits and Call Bodies in Prelude for near-double action economy: spend Psionic to copy an Administration card → gain 2 Materials via Call Spirits → spend 1 Material to spawn 2 ships via Call Bodies → move them via Slipstream Drive.
- Leave the Empire as soon as it is practical — Imperial ships block Banner placement and the Admiral can spread them deliberately to fence you out.
- Warlord benefits you doubly: it clears Trophies (recycling your limited Banner and ship supply) and is naturally easy to pursue while clearing planets for placement.
- Blight does not block Banner placement but destroys unguarded Banners at the Intermission; leave at least one fresh ship at any Banner you want to carry into Act 3.
- Don't trigger Outrage on Material or Psionic — these fuel Call Spirits and Call Bodies. Scan for multiple vulnerable planets of the same type before clearing with Weapons.

**Key kit:**
- **Song of the Banner** — the engine of the whole fate; unlocks Banner movement, Call Spirits (2 resources from a Banner planet), and Call Bodies (2 fresh ships at a Banner). Use it every turn.
- **Slipstream Drive** — mobility is everything for Banner placement; build armor for it at a Relic planet immediately.
- **Defense Array** — deters rivals from attacking your Flagship to destroy Banners; value scales with the number of Starport Upgrades/Armor slots on the Flagship board.
- **Tractor Beam** — prevents resource theft, relevant once you're hoarding Materials/Psionics to sustain the Call cycle.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — leave the Commonwealth with your very first action by battling a Free city; Armistice blocks harm to Free buildings and the crowded Commonwealth kills your Banner placement options. Staying even one chapter is crippling.
- **Admiral** — worst matchup in the faction. If staying in the Empire, use Imperial ships as cannon fodder: max Assault and Raid dice to assign self-hits to them, destroy them, then plant Banners in the next Prelude. If you go Outlaw, clear Imperial ships relentlessly — they block placement wherever they spread. Rogue Admirals (if you can secure them) solves both problems.
- **Warden** — Fiefs make your Flagship a Vassal when present in that cluster. Secure Claims of Nobility and hold it to stall Fief expansion. Move your Flagship into a Fief only as a deterrence tactic; send regular ships in without the Flagship to destroy cities without Provoking Outrage.

## Act 3 — Oppress and Dominate *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two Grand Ambitions — (1) no Rival wins Tyrant, and (2) more Banners on the map than the current chapter number. Priorities run in inverse order as the act progresses: early chapters focus on oppressing Rivals via captives; later chapters increasingly require Banner protection to meet the rising chapter threshold (Chapter 4 requires 5 Banners). Prefer Tyrant is never declared at all — your grand ambition is auto-satisfied and you preserve your captive stockpile.

- Attack with Skirmish dice to damage without destroying, then Pacify via Happy Hosts to sweep all damaged ships as captives in one action; this shrinks rival fleets far more efficiently than killing.
- Spend Fuel + Slipstream Drive to move to multiple systems with damaged ships, then spend one Relic to trigger Happy Hosts globally — one action captures across the whole map.
- After scoring Tyrant, you keep one captive per Banner (controlled or uncontrolled); retain a captive from each Rival to keep Mind Managers fully active into the next chapter.
- Drop Banners opportunistically as you roam; they serve the grand ambition threshold, enable Happy Hosts retention, and sustain the Song of the Banner network.
- Declare Tyrant early when grand ambition values are low — you start the following chapter already ahead and opponents haven't built captive pools yet.
- Avoid Weapon or Psionic Outrage at all costs; both Mind Managers and Happy Hosts are lost to it.

**Key kit:**
- **Happy Hosts** — captures all damaged ships in systems you control; keeps captives across chapter end equal to Banners on the map. The engine for shrinking rival fleets and staying ahead in Tyrant.
- **Mind Managers** — with a captive from each Rival: look at and swap one card from each rival's hand per Influence action. Use it to strip 3s (Tyrant declarations), drain high-pip action cards, or flood hands with 7s to bottleneck rivals entirely.
- **Relics** — trigger Happy Hosts' Pacify remotely; pair with Fuel and Slipstream Drive for board-wide captive sweeps after Blight Crises.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — still the worst matchup. Without Rogue Admirals you cannot control Imperial ships in the Empire, cratering efficiency and Banner placement. Leave the Empire or secure Rogue Admirals; either way, attack Imperial ships before the fleet grows.
- **Planet Breaker** — shift priorities hard; hit them early and often before their Flagship scales up. Happy Hosts is more important than Mind Managers to protect; losing both is near-fatal. Do not let Planet-Eater Loose trigger — it causes Psionic and Weapon Outrage simultaneously.
- **Survivalist** — the only fate that can seriously contest Tyrant without declaring it themselves. Use Mind Managers to strip other rivals' 3s, preventing anyone from declaring Tyrant at all. Destroy Blight rather than Bunkers to hamper Survivalist's engine.
- **Blight Speaker / Naturalist** — both thrive in the desolate, Blight-heavy board you prefer and can direct Blight at your Banners. Contest their system control early; Blight Crises are also prime Happy Hosts opportunities if you control the council's timing.
- **Peacekeeper** — Peaceful clusters protect existing Banners from destruction but prevent new ones and block Happy Hosts combos. If you already hold 5+ Banners, let them run; if you need more, keep a Weapon reserve to break Peace when necessary.

# Conspirator — Strategy Primer

The Conspirator is a C fate built around predicting, not winning, ambitions. Rather than declaring ambitions in the normal sense, you place conspiracy tokens on them — each unrevealed token on an undeclared ambition advances your objective passively, and each correct prediction on a declared ambition advances it at scoring. The core tension is between flooding the zone with tokens to build a passive base and making accurate predictions that hold up when rivals declare. Rivals can foil you by guessing your tokens (only when they secure a Vox card or a card carrying your agents), but a wrong guess gives you an advance, so the guessing game generally favours you. Farseers — which lets you look at an opponent's hand each time you place a conspiracy, then swap a card — is the engine that makes your predictions reliable rather than lucky.

---

## Act 3 — Read the Table, Flood the Zone *(Complexity 5/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 9 times (3p) or 6 times (4p) by holding conspiracy tokens on undeclared ambitions and predicting declared ones correctly. The low threshold can be rushed, but the objective can also grow if predictions go wrong, so discipline matters.

- Build a passive base of 2–3 conspiracies on ambitions rivals are *not* competing for; these accumulate chapter to chapter without requiring you to do anything.
- Place conspiracies as early in each chapter as possible — ambition markers are consumed over time, and you can only place via "declaration" while markers remain.
- Use Farseers' passive every time you place a conspiracy: knowing an opponent's hand lets you predict who will win an ambition and swap away cards that could disrupt it.
- Spread tokens across multiple ambitions rather than concentrating; if all your tokens sit on one or two ambitions that get declared, you restart from scratch next chapter.
- Avoid obvious predictions — they are easy to guess and easy for rivals to collectively deny. Obscure predictions that you quietly engineer are far harder to foil.
- If a conspiracy on an undeclared ambition gets threatened, you can place a second token as a bluff: opponents must guess correctly *and* pick the right token, and a wrong guess advances you regardless.
- Don't neglect board position: a robust fleet and tax base are what let you make the late-chapter swings that validate otherwise improbable predictions.

**Key kit:**
- **Conspiracies** — the core mechanism: replaces ambition declaration with token placement, giving you a second placement path (sacrifice a guild card after securing a court card). Use both paths freely; if a guild card's in-hand utility isn't obvious, spending it to place a conspiracy is usually correct.
- **Farseers** — look at one opponent's hand each time you place a conspiracy, then swap a card. The passive is where most of the value lives: knowing suit composition and top cards lets you predict outcomes and surgically remove the one card that could upset them. Save the discard-for-extra-turn ability for moments when you need a final push to validate a key prediction.
- **Galactic Bards** *(court card)* — lets you place a conspiracy without leading the trick, breaking the dependency on holding initiative early in a chapter. Almost always worth raiding; expect rivals to feel the same way.
- **Secret Order** *(court card)* — holding initiative early is critical for flooding the zone before markers run out; Secret Order lets you lead with a high card, place a conspiracy, and potentially maintain initiative to do it again.
- **Populist Demands** *(court card)* — secures a conspiracy placement and can trigger a crisis to return a marker to play, creating an additional placement window.
- **Union / Call to Action cards** — extra turns let you make final-chapter moves that validate predictions and enter the next chapter with initiative, placing conspiracies immediately.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Believer** — their Prophet and doctrine cards restrict which ambitions can be declared and when, limiting your placement windows and locking some ambitions behind Faithful action cards. Counter: place conspiracies via the guild-card sacrifice path on "undeclarable" ambitions where they sit safely; use Farseers to steal Faithful cards rivals would need to declare those ambitions.
- **Steward** — Dealmakers makes them a prolific court securer and therefore a frequent guesser. Keep curveball predictions ready; preventing them from holding First Regent (which feeds their court dominance) is worthwhile collateral pressure.
- **Warden** — Feudal Courts lets rivals engineer who can influence which court cards, potentially setting up easy Vox card secures to enable guessing or cutting off your own court access. Counter by either concentrating in a cluster or two to protect key cards, or maintaining broad presence to stay competitive across the whole court.
- **Partisan** *(pivot)* — a Partisan-turned-Conspirator is exceptionally strong: Partisan Seizing lets you seize initiative via outrage rather than card spend, Galactic Bards lets you place conspiracies without leading, and The People's Hero lightens the outrage cost. This pivot dominates the early-chapter initiative battle and floods the zone faster than any other entry path.

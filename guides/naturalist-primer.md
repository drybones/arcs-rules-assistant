# Naturalist — Strategy Primer

The Naturalist enters in Act 3 as the Blight's custodian: coexist with fresh Blight, shield it from crises using Psionics, and let it punish anyone who intrudes. Your objective is narrow and achievable — controlling ~4 systems with fresh Blight each chapter end — but wholly dependent on two things you don't produce yourself: enough Blight on the board, and an unbroken Psionic supply. Lose either and the Blight stops being your ally.

---

## Act 3 — The Blight Is Watching *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Control systems with fresh Blight at chapter end; advance 1 per system. Target is 18/16/14 advances at 2/3/4 players — roughly 4 Blighted systems per chapter. This is fast and achievable; you can complete it by chapter 2 in favourable games.

- Spend Psionics to activate Blight Society every crisis — this is non-negotiable. Hoard at least two Psionics in your 3-key slots at all times.
- Use the final turn of each chapter to sweep into fresh Blight systems that open up; Fuel and extra action cards make this possible.
- If you can hold First Regent, do so — Imperial systems count for your objective and you avoid fighting the fleet. If you can't, leave the Empire and battle Imperial ships directly.
- Consolidate your Blighted systems into one or two clusters to make Blight Fury strikes harder to aim and reduce the Psionic drain of defending them.
- Use gates: place damaged Blight in gates via Spore Guides to clog catapult paths and slow rivals down.
- Blightkin can threaten a Power victory if you begin Act 3 in a strong Power position — declare it early and often in that case. If you're behind on Power, leave it undeclared; the ambition is a liability if a rival steals it, as you advance nothing that chapter.

**Key kit:**
- **Blight Society** — the entire strategy. Spend 1 Psionic to block all Blight damage in every system with your pieces during a crisis; lets you sit in fresh Blight safely while rivals take damage. Also lets you repair and move Blight you control.
- **Spore Guides** — place a damaged Blight semi-randomly via a build action; spreads Blight when the board runs thin and clogs gates for free. Less essential than Blight Society but valuable when Blight supply is low.
- **Loyal Empaths** *(Partisan Act 1 success)* — spend any resource as a Psionic, including to trigger Blight Society. Effectively eliminates Weakness 1; secure this above almost everything else.
- **Imperial Protectors** *(Steward Act 1 success)* — if you hold First Regent, this makes Imperial systems immune to Blight crises and removes the need to spend Psionics there at all. Only worthwhile if you can hold First Regent consistently.
- **Blight Hunters / Blight Reapers** *(Blight Speaker Act 2 success)* — both are bad for you in rival hands. Secure them to deny access, not to use.
- **Psionic Cartel** *(Magnate after Act 1 or Act 2 failure)* — dangerous in a rival's hands (after-scoring effect strips all Psionics from every player, crippling you). Secure it yourself and immediately spend its prelude effect to clear it from the game.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — leave the Empire. They will hold First Regent with Dealmakers, Imperial Protectors kills your Blight crises in Imperial systems, and you can't fight Regent ships. Go Outlaw and attack Imperial ships directly.
- **Blight Speaker** — your strangest rival. They compete fiercely for Psionics and will use Blight Fury to hit your clusters. Consolidate your systems to make targeting harder; their Blight Fury costs them Psionics too, so hoarding more than them wins the war of attrition. Their cards added after Act 2 success (Blight Hunters, Blight Reapers, Blight Looms) make the court hostile — secure the Hunters and Reapers before anyone else does.
- **Admiral** — Martial Law means Imperial ships in your systems can't be commandeered and cut you off from catapult access unless you hold Rogue Admirals. Either secure both copies of Rogue Admirals and First Regent, or leave the Empire and fight the fleet outright.
- **Magnate** — they will target the Psionic supply. Hold at least one Psionic city, keep extras in your 3-key slots, and know when to raid Psionics if the supply dries up.
- **Pacifist** — generally favourable. Their Act 2 Witness placement expands the Psionic supply considerably, making it hard for others to starve you, and their pacifism means they must engage you non-militarily.
- **Planet Breaker** — coordinate watching for a Psionic-planet destruction paired with a crisis; that combo can wipe your entire position in one round. If it happens, stabilise, get Heartworld Refugees, and survive — but note there are only three Psionic planets in the galaxy.

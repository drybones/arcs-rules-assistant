# Founder — Strategy Primer

The Founder begins as the Empire's saboteur and ends as its replacement. Every act follows the same throughline: undermine Imperial authority, build an alternative power structure outside its reach, and use cooperative mechanics to outmanoeuvre rivals who are still playing the Empire's game. You will never be the most powerful piece on the board, but you don't need to be — your leverage comes from controlling who can fight whom, not from winning fights outright. Diplomacy, timing, and board presence through sheer ship numbers are your real weapons.

---

## Act 1 — Break Free *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 3 per ambition won (as Outlaw) and 1 per system controlled at chapter end. Winning ambitions is the bigger driver; system control compounds quietly. Tycoon is your default target — Fuel and Material cards you want anyway double as Tycoon icons. Warlord is viable if you're fighting frequently; avoid Keeper and Empath unless the field is empty.

- Become an Outlaw on your first ambition declaration unless there is a specific reason to delay (a nearby catapult gate you need, or a rival poised to battle you immediately). Never get stuck in the Empire into Chapter 2.
- Use Parade Fleets every time you have initiative — declare an ambition to place 2 ships at any Free city, even if you cannot win that ambition. Ship volume is your resource.
- Open on a Fuel planet on the empire's edge; two starports over city/starport is worth considering for mobility. Sell or trade your starting buildings in the first Summit — they are liabilities once you're Outlaw.
- Hybrid your playstyle: establish a stable position in the outer clusters first (evasive), then switch to harassing Imperial ships once you can absorb retaliation. Overextending before you have a safe base stalls your engine.
- Keep the Empire weak: petition the Council to keep it In Session, push for Imperial Demand over Policy of Escalation, and encourage Regent dissent. Fewer Imperial ships means fewer obstacles and easier Trophy hunting for rivals (which weakens the Empire further).
- Seize initiative at most once per chapter; the card cost is small, but timing matters — use it to declare before a rival can, not as a habit.

**Key kit:**
- **Parade Fleets** — places 2 ships at any Free city on ambition declaration; also the mechanism for going Outlaw. Declare whenever you hold initiative, period.
- **Ambition-granting cards** (Tycoon's Ambition, Populist Demands, etc.) — each one is another Parade Fleets trigger; grab any you see.
- **Mining Interest** — played just before Tycoon scores, it is usually a free ambition win and fuels your Build actions.
- **Gatekeepers** — Fuel icon for Tycoon; discarding it drops a ship in every gate, potentially controlling several systems as a chapter closes.
- **Skirmishers** — reroll skirmish dice; valuable when clearing Blight and when fighting with large fleets where ship losses hurt less proportionally.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — your direct nemesis; their objective feeds on you as a Trophy source and demands a strong Empire. Strike first: go Outlaw at a moment when you can immediately battle them (ideally with a Weapon on a high-pip Aggression lead). This is an act-long war — keep your ship count up and never leave yourself unable to recover from retaliation.
- **Steward** — a more indirect rivalry played out in the court and through diplomacy. Argue to other players that Edict frequency under the Steward drains their resources, and that the Steward's kit (Dealmakers, Imperial Authority) is a table-wide threat requiring collective resistance.
- **Caretaker** — Crises triggering more often is dangerous if you're camped in the Blight. Seek alliance to claim Golems for yourself; they help win ambitions and protect ships.

## Act 2 — Found the Commonwealth *(Complexity 4/5, Difficulty 1/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 2 per Commonwealth member when the Commonwealth wins the Commonwealth Ambition, and 1 per chapter per Free city not Empire-controlled. Winning the Commonwealth Ambition once with a full membership can nearly complete the objective. Build Free cities widely, then time a single well-set-up Commonwealth Ambition to close it out — you have breathing room this act.

- Start Act 2 as last in Power if you can manage it; controlling Call for Armistice is worth more than the Power you give up.
- Declare the Commonwealth Ambition often, but never on an ambition a non-member can win overwhelmingly — the non-member bonus (double the combined 1st/2nd Power with no cap) can hand someone 20+ Power in a single score.
- Build Free cities broadly across many clusters with Book of Liberation; Free starports near your fleet act as "Loyal" starports without the raid liability. After 3 Loyal cities, replace further building with Free construction.
- Raid freely now — Book of Liberation clears Outrage. Never accumulate Fuel Outrage; Parade Fleets cannot be lost.
- Use a willing scapegoat: engineer exactly one player leaving the Commonwealth to draw rival aggression and unite remaining members. Having them leave now removes the late-game surprise exit threat.
- Keep battling Imperial ships — Armistices only block harm against Rivals/Free buildings, not against the Empire.

**Key kit:**
- **Book of Liberation** — build Free cities and starports, clear Outrage to raid freely; the foundation of your Act 2 board state.
- **Founder of the Commonwealth** — Title that lets you attach the Commonwealth Ambition marker when declaring; use it almost every declaration.
- **Call for Armistice / Armistice cards** — prevents Commonwealth members from harming each other or Free buildings while in court, trapping them in the Commonwealth. Place on cards where you already lead in agents; a 3-agent lead on a low-value card (Song of Freedom) is ideal.
- **Empire Falls** (if Steward failed Act 1) — guard the Crisis text from being secured; if it triggers, the Empire collapses and every disadvantage of being Outlaw vanishes.
- **Political Intrigue** (if you succeeded Act 1) — good general court control; do not let its Crisis effect fire, as rivals holding your Favors undermines your Armistice lock.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — still your chief threat; Armistice locks are your best counter. If they leave the Commonwealth on turn 1, they will hunt you relentlessly. Call for Armistice immediately and try to keep them in.
- **Warden** — you can build Free cities inside their Fiefs; sell that service for Favors rather than giving it away. Acquiring a Fief of your own improves your ability to take Captives while Armistices block direct fighting.
- **Pacifist** — a natural long-term ally; they crave Armistice protection as much as you do and want Free starports for their Witnesses. Coordinate to keep Armistices in the court; be careful declaring Commonwealth Empath if they can overwhelm the value.
- **Magnate** — a Monopoly-heavy Magnate will be tempted to exit and win ambitions alone; keep them in by demonstrating the taxing flexibility Free cities give them, or weaken their Monopoly count before it becomes insurmountable.
- **Hegemon / Pirate / Planet Breaker** — all need to harm Free cities and will leave; use them as the designated scapegoat rather than fighting to keep them in.

## Act 3 — Peace by Force *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score grand ambitions by winning the Commonwealth Ambition — you cannot score at all without it. Two grand ambitions are always live: Free cities not Empire-controlled, and a condition tied to Commonwealth wins. Power is no longer halved after Intermission, so a single non-member who wins the Commonwealth Ambition as an outsider (double Power, no cap) can end the game. Locking members in via Armistice is now existential, not merely convenient.

- Mass Call for Armistice gives you your own Armistice call without needing to be last in Power; use it proactively. Keeping one Armistice in the court permanently is viable.
- If all 10 agents are placed on a single Armistice card and all rivals are members, no one can ever leave (no ransacking possible, no securing). Only do this if you have a Power lead or a crippled board state — it locks you out of new Guild cards and is vulnerable to Diplomatic Fiasco.
- Replace Loyal cities with Free cities (via Song of Freedom or by allowing early attacks); cities are ransack vectors that lose you court control. Protect the ones you keep with ships and threat of Outrage.
- Disrupt rival A/B Fates more than C Fates — you score slowly, so preventing rivals from scoring matters more than accelerating your own total. Use the Commonwealth Ambition on ambitions A/B members would otherwise win individually to equalise Power gain across the table.
- If everyone has left the Commonwealth, abandon your grand ambitions entirely; the risk of a non-member win vastly outweighs any scoring upside.

**Key kit:**
- **Mass Call for Armistice** — upgraded Edict; you no longer depend on being last in Power to place an Armistice. The core of your Act 3 control engine.
- **Freedom Fighters** — destroys all damaged Rival and Imperial ships at a fresh Free city without needing to battle; strong when many ships are already damaged and your fleet is spread thin. Does not generate Trophies.
- **Spirit of Freedom** — court control and easier Guild card acquisition; aids the agent advantage that keeps Armistices locked down.
- **Song of Freedom** — convert a Loyal city to Free; attach an Armistice to it and lock it down with 3 agents before securing to convert multiple of your cities cheaply.
- **Dealmakers** (Steward lineage) — must not fall into a rival's hands. If someone holds it, your entire court-lock strategy collapses. Contest it at all costs or Outrage it out of the game.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — now controls planets via Imperial ships, directly opposing your Free city grand ambition. Keep blocking gates with ships, keep building Free cities, and keep battling Imperial ships. Alert other Regents that Martial Law cuts them off too — frame it as a shared problem.
- **Believer** — Dominant Faith restricts which ambitions can be declared with which cards; if the Doctrine blocks your declaration suit, you cannot place the Commonwealth Ambition and cannot score. Destroy their cities between Armistices or threaten enough to force the Doctrine onto a suit you hold.
- **Survivalist** — takes many Captives, which drains rival agent pools and hands you court dominance indirectly; rarely contests Armistice cards. Negotiate to keep them from targeting your own agents.
- **Advocate** — attaches cards to court cards, which can block Armistice placement if slots fill up. Their grand ambition is also hard-countered by a single Armistice in the court — use that leverage.
- **Magnate** — Monopoly scrapping reshapes ambition competition; avoid letting them hold Fuel or Weapon Monopolies. Contest Monopolies yourself or push other players to do so.

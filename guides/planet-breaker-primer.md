# Planet Breaker — Strategy Primer

The Planet Breaker is the campaign's designated threat. Your entire arc is built around assembling the Planet Hammer from six scattered fragments and then detonating planets — forcing table-wide outrage that strips Guild cards from every Rival simultaneously. You are an obvious target from the moment you reveal your Fate, and managing that attention through fleet positioning, diplomacy, and timing the Accelerated Timetable is as important as collecting the fragments themselves.

---

## Act 2 — Forge the Hammer *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 12 times. Picking up each fragment advances you 1 and places a free ship; breaking a planet is the act-completing move. Stay Regent: the Truce Law and Command the Fleet make reaching guarded fragments far easier, and leaving the Empire before the Hammer is assembled makes the whole task significantly harder.

- Prioritise collecting fragments over everything else in early chapters — ambition play is secondary until the Hammer is assembled.
- Use two complementary approaches: small raiding parties (1 Loyal ship + 1 Imperial ship) for distant fragments, and a death ball for contested clusters. A hybrid — raiding parties in one direction, death ball in the other — is usually most efficient.
- Keep Slipstream Drive alive at all costs; use Material + Fuel/Psionic combos to enter a system, collect a fragment, and exit in one efficient sequence.
- To break a planet, seize initiative on the Prepare turn (ideally when last in turn order), then open the following round with an Aggression lead or spend a Weapon to resolve Breaking Worlds before Rivals can position — this is the Accelerated Timetable.
- Aim to break a planet with at least two buildings; those buildings carry forward to Act 3.
- Target an isolated, low-traffic planet for your first break; reduce the odds of a Rival fleet thwarting you.
- Secure at least one Weapon before the third chapter — you need it for Breaking Worlds.
- Do not outrage Material or Weapon; both are load-bearing through the end of the campaign.
- After completing your objective, consider breaking a second planet to pre-load Act 3 progress.

**Key kit:**
- **Sycophants** (protected Weapon Guild card) — places 2 ships at any planet of the outrage type whenever you provoke outrage, once per turn. Doubles as a free deployment tool before the Hammer is assembled; also survives Weapon outrage, so it won't be lost when you inevitably trigger it.
- **Hammer Fragments / Breaking Worlds** — your core mechanic. Repair Hammer collects a fragment and gives a free ship; once all six are collected you gain the Planet Hammer lore card and unlock the two-step Break World action.
- **Slipstream Drive (Flagship Upgrade)** — rebuild immediately if destroyed; all efficient fragment collection depends on it.
- **Defense Array (Flagship Upgrade)** — second priority upgrade; every Rival will battle you during Breaking Worlds, and this deters aggression.
- **Control Array (Flagship Upgrade)** — resource combos it enables make fragment collection and planet-breaking harder to predict and interrupt.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — Imperial ship spread makes fragment collection easier as a Regent; become an Outlaw and that spread becomes a wall. Stay in the Empire and exploit the Truce Law.
- **Founder** — leave the Commonwealth immediately; if an Armistice takes effect before you leave, harming Free buildings (including Breaking Worlds) becomes impossible. A single battle action against a Free building gets you out — you can even collect a Raid die for a resource.
- **Admiral** — wants to force you out of the Empire via Imperial Demand. Don't budge: Imperial protections are worth the drain. Contest Rogue Admirals aggressively — the Admiral wants the same card. Stack agents on it; if necessary, Ransack them for it.
- **Partisan** — Partisan Seizing lets you use the Accelerated Timetable without discarding a card (the outrage you'll trigger anyway serves as the seize). Be aware this consumes that seize opportunity. Prefer the Partisan to fail this Act: their Act 3 lore card can simply turn off your Weapons.
- **Pacifist** — must fail this Act. Their Act 3 negotiation action lets Rivals clear outrage trivially, gutting your grand ambitions. Deny their Empath declaration by exhausting ambition markers early; harass them on the board to push a pivot.
- **Peacekeeper** — better off failing here too; their Act 3 Ceasefires impose a Weapon tax on every battle. Poach their Imperial ships and raid their Weapons if needed.

## Act 3 — Break the Reach *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two grand ambitions, both checked at each chapter end: (1) all Rivals have outrage, and (2) buildings on your Breaking Worlds card exceed the current chapter number (so five buildings by the end of chapter 4). Neither grand ambition scores in a chapter where any Rival wins Warlord. Controlling Warlord — or suppressing it entirely — is the central strategic axis.

- Never declare Warlord before chapter 4; the 20-point grand ambition payoff in the final chapter outweighs the ambition's immediate value, and a declared Warlord wipes the boards.
- Declare non-Warlord ambitions to force Rivals to choose between Warlord and what they actually want.
- Count 4s and 7s as Rivals play them; if none appear in your hand and few remain in the deck, reserve Weapons to refill a wiped Trophy box rather than spending them offensively.
- Never call a Summit — Rivals can consolidate Trophies to gift a Warlord win. Deny Summits by securing the Imperial Council ahead of an Event card, or seize initiative immediately after an Event is played.
- Deny Populist Demands (starting court card); it gives Rivals an extra Warlord declaration window. Stack agents on it; you can't be Ransacked from your Flagship, so this is relatively safe.
- Break roughly one building per chapter to stay on pace; single-building planets are less defended than two-building targets.
- Do not break Material or Weapon planets unless you already have five buildings on the card — you need both resource types to sustain combat and planet-breaking.
- Use the Cloak and Dagger approach: Surpass and Prepare on a "decoy" planet to bait Rival fleets out of position, then pivot to the true target the following turn.
- When free buildings run low, shift to the Going for the Jugular approach — death ball into a Rival cluster. Use spare actions to move inconvenient Imperial ships out of the system before resolving Breaking Worlds.

**Key kit:**
- **Sycophants** — still your only kit card; now places ships at fresh planets after every outrage trigger, fuelling both Trophy collection and map presence for Warlord contests.
- **Galactic Bards / Lesser Regent / Liaison cards** — allow ambition declarations outside the normal window; use single-use cards when only one ambition marker remains to lock Warlord out for the chapter.
- **Arms Interest (Peacekeeper Act 2 success)** — converts spare build actions into Weapons; also lets you preemptively steal Rivals' Weapons via its Prelude effect.
- **Unstable Warrior / Golem Worship (Caretaker successes)** — grants the Warrior Golem for remote Trophy collection; Blight Crises generate easy targets.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — their Free buildings across the Reach are ideal targets; use them to pace your grand ambition. They will retaliate — keep your Flagship insulated and strike before they can mass a response.
- **Admiral** — without Rogue Admirals, Martial Law means you lose control of Imperial ships entirely. If the Imperial ships aren't positioned usefully for your planet-breaking plan, leave the Empire. Avoid destroying the Admiral's Loyal ships early; let them accumulate, then destroy a Weapon planet in the back half of the Act — this discards Imperial Officers and all Rogue Admirals at the table, severely handicapping both their grand ambitions.
- **Partisan** — break planets immediately; the first time the Partisan surpasses to gain initiative, Sower of Division can permanently remove your ability to spend Weapons (and block Sycophants from triggering). If that happens, every Aggression card drawn must go toward planet-breaking.
- **Magnate** — hold the Weapon Monopoly to prevent War Profiteering from letting the Magnate use your Weapon icons against you and to block Weapon scrapping.
- **Advocate** — their Guild cards count double toward ambitions and they no longer discard them when outraging; you'll need extra planet breaks to pressure their tableau, plus raiding their cards directly. Prioritise whichever is more achievable given your current position.
- **Pacifist** — Rivals can freely clear outrage, undermining grand ambition 1. Break planets near chapter end to minimise the window for clearance; deny Summits aggressively; keep the Imperial Council on its Decided side when possible.
- **Peacekeeper** — every battle costs a Weapon just to deal damage; stockpile Weapons and always maintain at least one in reserve to raid theirs back. Don't let them accumulate.
- **Gate Wraith** — The Dead Live clears Trophy boxes regardless of Warlord; Blight kills and building destructions are your only reliable Trophy accumulation. Destroy your own ships more freely as attacker — they return at chapter start anyway.

# Partisan — Strategy Primer

The Partisan is a trick-taking fate at its core: every act revolves around winning rounds, holding or regaining the initiative marker, and out-maneuvering rivals in the court. Where other fates build engines of ships or resources, the Partisan builds a Guild card tableau — amassing suits across Act 1, weaponising them in Act 2 through People's Hero, and defending plurality in Act 3. Partisan Seizing is the spine of the entire campaign: a tool that advances your objective, outrages resources, and sets up future acts — but also a law that reshapes the whole table once Act 2 begins. Play it too timidly and you fall behind; play it recklessly and you burn your own foundations.

---

## Act 1 — Seize the Moment *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Earn 10–12 advancements (player-count dependent) through a mix of winning tricks (gaining the initiative marker) and winning declared ambitions. Win at least one ambition per chapter — two wins in Chapter 1 is optimal. Aim for 3 advancements from ambitions before outrage starts closing them off. Use Partisan Seizing from Chapter 2 onwards to add the remaining 2–3 advancements and to open up ransacking opportunities. End the act with a plurality of Guild cards in at least one outraged suit.

- Outrage 2–3 resources, following the resource hierarchy: Material and Fuel first, then Relics or Weapons, Psionic last. Never outrage a suit where you've already accumulated Guild cards.
- Seize before collecting Guild cards of that suit — outraging the wrong suit after building a tableau can undo multiple acts of work.
- Relics are worth outraging early if it unlocks a Relic guild plurality for Act 2; they are also the easiest to clear with People's Hero and a prime target when the Steward or Advocate is competing for them.
- You only advance when *gaining* the initiative marker, not holding it — intentionally lose the marker to create opportunities to reclaim it. Coordinate with the next player in turn order: offer them a low-suited trick they can play through in exchange for passing it back.
- Track played cards and card numbers across the chapter to time Informants swaps and predict when a high card can win.
- Stay within the Empire if possible; First Regent gives free defence, easier ambition wins, and taxation leverage. Go Outlaw only if the Empire looks close to collapse.

**Key kit:**
- **Partisan Seizing** — seizes the initiative at the cost of outrage; saves a card compared to normal seizing and advances your objective by 1 each use. The threat alone forces rivals to over-spend on court cards.
- **Informants** — swap your highest card with a rival's; upgrades a weak hand and reveals information about their options. Most valuable when holding low cards (1–3) or when paired with the Believer's Faithful cards.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — has superior resource access via Imperial Authority and Dealmakers. Contest First Regent aggressively to block their ambition wins and deny them Dealmakers. Target Relic or Weapon cities for outrage to raid them and secure their guild cards.
- **Advocate** — your long-run nemesis. A passing Advocate adds a second Diplomatic Fiasco to the deck, which becomes increasingly punishing through Acts 2 and 3. Outrage resources matching their cities and prepare for a raid in Act 2 if they cannot be dislodged now.
- **Believer** — a positive matchup. Faithful cards they attach to court cards give you extra action cards; pick them up immediately. Informants is especially strong here: you know exactly which Faithful cards rivals hold by their card backs.

## Act 2 — People's Hero *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Hold the initiative marker when playing action cards to advance. You need a sustained run of consecutive tricks — play your highest cards (5/6/7) in sequence once you seize. Triggering People's Hero is the single most important event of the act: declare an ambition while holding a plurality of Guild cards in a suit that matches one of your outrages, remove that outrage, and advance once per Guild card you hold regardless of suit. Trigger it as early as possible — even without expecting to win the ambition. Two triggers nearly guarantees the act.

- Maintain, don't burn: hold Guild cards from Act 1 into Act 2 rather than using prelude effects. Aim to start Act 2 with 4–6 Guild cards.
- Shift the resource hierarchy: Relic and Psionic are now most valuable (Galactic Bards depends on Relic; Informants on Psionic). Outrage Material and Fuel first.
- Partisan Seizing is now a universal law — every rival can use it. Earlier in turn order gets priority, so you can no longer guarantee seizure. Galactic Bards compensates: it lets you declare ambitions on a surpass or pivot, triggering People's Hero without holding the lead.
- Agent economy becomes the dominant currency — resources are scarce as outrage stacks up. Tax rival cities, trade for agents, and lean into First Regent if you can sustain it. Do not give favors away; you will need every agent in Act 3 for Sower of Division.
- Cap your Guild card count at 6 — Diplomatic Fiasco (added if Advocate passed Act 1) penalises players with more Guild cards than agents.
- Protect your infrastructure in Relic and Psionic zones to deter rivals from outraging those suits and raiding you.

**Key kit:**
- **People's Hero** — removes an outrage when you declare an ambition with the matching Guild plurality; triggers Burnish Your Image to advance once per Guild card held. The entire act is built around firing this.
- **Galactic Bards (Partisan success Act 1)** — declare an ambition when surpassing or pivoting, making People's Hero accessible without holding the initiative marker. Only 1 key — protect it.
- **Loyal Cards (Partisan success Act 1)** — treat any resource as Loyal during prelude; ignore outrage on that suit. Static effects that cannot be removed. Prioritise any suit where no rival holds a matching protected card — monopolising a suit now carries into Act 3.
- **Dealmakers (Steward failure Act 1)** — the best Guild-card engine in the game; protected, so it cannot be raided once secured.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Advocate** — adds Diplomatic Fiasco and gains Demand abilities that can strip your Guild cards for favour. Counter by holding First Regent to return those favors, and use ransacking over influence contests to preserve agents. Never give them a suit they don't already have.
- **Founder** — Book of Liberation activates outrage removal more easily than People's Hero, giving them initiative control at will. Political Intrigue can drain agents you need for Act 3. If Armistice cards appear, decide whether that stability serves you — it usually doesn't.
- **Warden** — becoming their Vassal drains your agent supply rapidly. Avoid it. The silver lining: your existing outrage softens the penalty for damaging their Seat — hit it whenever you can.

## Act 3 — Hold the Court *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two Grand Ambitions, scored at each chapter end: hold more Guild cards than each rival, and hold the initiative marker at chapter end. Both are easily disrupted by Partisan Seizing — rivals can steal the marker in the final round with a single two-card spend. The act is about anticipating and neutralising those last-minute seizures. Go Outlaw immediately: Imperial Demand and the volatile First Regent position under Guilds Decry Terror cost more than they return, and the Truce Law blocks the aggressive play you need.

- Guilds Decry Terror (added on Act 2 success) attaches Diplomatic Fiasco to the Imperial Council and flips it to In Session whenever an edict fires — expect it repeatedly. Keep your agent count above your Guild card count at all times, or be ready to recall agents quickly.
- Use Diplomatic Fiasco offensively: tax or ransack rivals' agents to push them below their Guild card count and force discards. Target the player most threatening your plurality first.
- Sower of Division — place an agent on a rival's empty outrage slot when you surpass to seize, blocking them from Partisan Seizing that slot. Use it early; trick-taking is volatile and you cannot rely on triggering it at will. In 4-player games its impact is diluted — don't deplete your agent supply chasing it.
- Surpassing with a 7 seizes the initiative immediately and does *not* trigger Sower of Division — plan around this edge case in final rounds.
- Dump low cards early to fish out higher numbers; lead with low cards to burn suit options from rivals' hands and bait out their high cards.
- Card-tracking from Acts 1–2 pays full dividends here: reconstruct rivals' hands through observed plays, and time high-card plays to deceive rather than reveal your hand.

**Key kit:**
- **Sower of Division** — blocks a rival from Partisan Seizing on a specific outrage slot when you surpass to seize. Targeted use in the penultimate round of a chapter can make your second Grand Ambition uncontestable.
- **People's Hero (carried from Act 2)** — still the engine for outrage recycling; lets you Partisan Seize again after clearing a slot. Losing a Guild card of that suit to the seizure is unavoidable — manage it.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Advocate** — directly competes for Guild card plurality. Their Guild Supremacy law doubles resource icon values for ambition scoring, drawing everyone into contested influence battles. Favour ransacking over influence contests to capture agents and avoid squandering your own. Mirror their suits where possible to nullify their Demand trigger.
- **Planet Breaker** — your Partisan Seizing law fulfils one of their Grand Ambitions outright. If they're already in a winning position, coordinate the table to declare Warlord every chapter and deny them the win; prepare for sustained galactic combat.
- **Hegemon** — Mind Managers lets them look through your hand and swap the card of their choice, dismantling your trick-taking game and denying you the initiative. Do not give them a captive under any circumstances.
- **Conspirator** — Farseers lets its holder peek and swap cards when an ambition is declared. If a Conspirator is present, raid them for it immediately — you want to control this effect, not fight it.
- **Pacifist** — Reconcile with the Outraged lets them remove your outrage tokens as favors, freeing up seizure slots for rivals and draining your agent supply. Work against them in Act 2 to avoid this interaction in Act 3.
- **Overlord** — benefits directly from Partisan Seizing (easy outrage accumulation). Counter: consistently call Edicts at summits to trigger The Reach Rejects Overlord and drain their power track before they fulfill their Grand Ambition.

# Peacekeeper — Strategy Primer

The Peacekeeper is a situational diplomat who wins not through force but through control of the one resource everyone else needs to fight: Weapons. The *Oath of Peace* closes off Warlord entirely, so your scoring comes from hoarding Weapon resources onto *Arsenal Keepers* and controlling Weapon planets — passive, hard-to-disrupt income that compounds quietly while rivals focus on each other. In Act 3 the whole strategy pivots to a map-wide area-control race built on the board presence you quietly assembled in Act 2. Peacekeeper rewards table talk and restraint; the fate's low profile is itself a strategic asset.

---

## Act 2 — The Arms Race *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance by holding Weapon resources on *Arsenal Keepers* (1 point each) and controlling Weapon planets at chapter end (2 points each). Prioritise locking all five Weapons onto *Arsenal Keepers* first — this is passive income nobody can easily stop and simultaneously starves rivals of Warlord fuel. Control two Weapon planets second. Third, spread presence to at least one system in every cluster to set up Act 3.

- Be First Regent and enforce Policy of Escalation — it collects Material, Fuel, or Weapons, any of which help you, and the Imperial Action places ships for free board presence.
- *Oath of Peace* blocks non-Blight trophy collection, not battling itself — use violence to police rivals and take Blight trophies for Summit bargaining chips; you can also trade Blight trophies for ships or cluster access when Warlord is declared.
- Avoid going Outlaw: controlling systems as an Outlaw is harder, and you'll need Regent status for Act 3. If forced Outlaw, negotiate re-entry before Intermission even at the cost of board position.
- Build a city on a Weapon planet and tax it every turn you can in the opening chapters; don't wait to spread until after you've locked the Weapons.
- Destroy rivals' starports at Weapon planets before end of Act — a city gives you control where a starport does not.

**Key kit:**
- **Oath of Peace** (Lore) — invalidates Warlord for you; use it as a bargaining chip to trade trophies for resources or board position when Warlord is active. Passing it to a Caretaker in negotiation scraps it entirely — strongly worth attempting if the opportunity arises.
- **Arsenal Keepers** (Guild) — stores Weapon resources off your board so they don't eat resource slots; resources here count toward your objective and can be filled via tax, raid, Imperial Demand, or Imperial Trust theft. The frozen pile also prevents Warlord from being a competitive ambition for others.
- **Court Enforcers** (added if Admiral played Act 1) — locks down the Court against rivals without your Weapon count; secures First Regent and generates captives for Tyrant. Secure immediately; it will be targeted.
- **Outrage Spreads** (Vox, if Partisan failed Act 1) — if Weapons Outrage triggers, you lose *Arsenal Keepers* and most of your objective progress. Secure and discard this card the moment it appears.
- **Loyal Marines** (Guild, if Partisan succeeded Act 1) — lets rivals act as if they hold Weapons even when you've hoarded them all, countering your core strategy. Secure it and discard it immediately.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — fights you for First Regent and uses Imperial ships to contest system control without needing Loyal pieces present. If Admiral is in the game, this matchup is severe; seriously consider a different fate.
- **Steward** — also wants First Regent and a strong Empire; contested but manageable if you got there first. Think twice before choosing Peacekeeper if Steward succeeded Act 1.
- **Pirate** — incentivised to steal resources and break up hoards; your Weapons pile will be a target. Lock them onto *Arsenal Keepers* as fast as possible to reduce the window.
- **Magnate** — competing for resource hoarding but has far more objectives to manage than you; if they hold the Weapons Monopoly you can still tax it back. Can be a natural ally.
- **Planet Breaker** — destroying any Weapon planet invalidates *Arsenal Keepers* and forces a C-fate pivot. Counter their objective actively; preventing even one planet destruction is critical.

## Act 3 — Peace, at Any Cost *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Grand Ambition 1 requires 2 clusters at Peace; Grand Ambition 2 requires 5 clusters at Peace. Clusters start at Peace if you controlled at least one system there at Act 2 end — the Act 2 spread milestone pays off here directly. Hoard all five Weapons back onto *Arsenal Keepers* in the first chapter actions; with all Weapons frozen, rivals cannot spend one to break a Ceasefire and your grand ambitions lock in. Score additional Power from *Peace Dividends* by controlling as many systems as possible across all peaceful clusters.

- Tax all Weapons back at the very start of Chapter 1 before rivals can move — the frozen pile prevents Ceasefires from flipping. If you pull this off with all clusters at Peace, your grand ambitions may be effectively complete before rivals react.
- Fight hard for First Regent — *Peace Dividends* scores for every player per system in peaceful clusters on each Edict trigger, but the First Regent scores most often on average. Ransack the Council rather than lose it, even if it costs resources.
- Choke the Court: enforce Policy of Peace to drain Relic and Psionic supplies, making it harder for rivals to secure the Council or Court cards. A slower Court keeps First Regent in your hands.
- If you cannot hoard all five Weapons, negotiate for them early; savvy rivals will withhold them knowing the scoring engine you're building. Offer points, board position, or Blight trophies rather than waiting to collect by force.
- If you end up Outlaw, *Empire Balks at Peace* becomes a live threat. Consolidate the Empire onto a small number of planets in one "at War" cluster and destroy it there rather than let rivals weaponise it against you.

**Key kit:**
- **Ceasefires** (Law) — the core constraint: any harm in a peaceful cluster costs the attacker a Weapon and flips the token to "at War." With no Weapons in play, your clusters are locked. Rivals who want to contest you are stuck spending resources they need for ambitions.
- **Peace Dividends** (Edict) — triggers on every Edict resolution: all players score 1 Power per system controlled in peaceful clusters. This is the reason to spread in Act 2 and the reason rivals won't simply let all clusters go to war. Make sure you score 2–4 more than each rival per trigger; let them benefit a little so they don't work against you.
- **Peace Accords** (Summit) — the fallback if clusters start "at War": call a Summit and convert a cluster to Peace if you control half of it. Requires ally consent if you lack the board position alone.
- **Empire Balks at Peace** (Edict) — only relevant if you're Outlaw; avoid that status.
- **Arms Interest** (Guild, added on Peacekeeper Act 2 success) — strong for you, potentially devastating if rivals hold it. Secure or remove it from the Court immediately.
- **Council Intrigue** (Vox, always present) — works against you if rivals use it; find a way to exploit it yourself and limit its use against your First Regent position.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — Martial Law blocks your use of the Empire entirely if they hold First Regent; combined with their direct First Regent competition, this is a near-automatic pivot away from Peacekeeper. If Admiral succeeded Act 2, pivot.
- **Steward** — adds *Govern with Authority* cards that reinforce their own First Regent position; harder to dislodge than in Act 2 but not an automatic counter. Board position from Act 2, current First Regent, and carried grudges all affect how dangerous this matchup is.
- **Naturalist** — also wants First Regent for the Blightkin ambition and can score Peace Dividends on top of completing their own objective. Ensure someone else wins Blightkin; fight them in isolated "at War" clusters to contain Blight without flipping peaceful ones.
- **Guardian** — competes for planet control via the Edenguard ambition (Fuel and Material planets). Use Imperial ships to hold the planets they need, or deny them ships. Ensure someone other than Guardian wins Edenguard.
- **Magnate** — if they scrap Weapon resources and the Weapons Monopoly in Act 3, that benefits you directly. A natural deal partner; negotiate so they pursue their objective without competing for your Weapons.
- **Planet Breaker** — cannot harm planets in peaceful clusters, so they're bottled up in "at War" regions. If they can still reach a Weapon planet in an "at War" cluster, the damage to your kit is severe; monitor their access and consider pivoting if they pose a credible threat.

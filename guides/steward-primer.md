# Steward — Strategy Primer

The Steward is the Empire's self-appointed custodian: a court-manipulation powerhouse whose kit is tuned entirely around holding First Regent and taxing everyone within reach of it. Dealmakers gives the Steward unmatched speed and flexibility in the Court — securing cards in a single turn, recycling rival agents, and generating a reliable supply of captives — but the fate's true balancing act is governing aggressively enough to win objectives without driving Regents into Outlaw status, since every future Act depends on a healthy Empire to tax.

---

## Act 1 — Steward the Court *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score 14 Power (4p) or 17 Power (3p) by winning ambitions while holding First Regent. Tying does not count. Keep a Relic city online to fuel Dealmakers; prioritise Tyrant and Tycoon since your court control feeds the former and Policy of Escalation's Material/Fuel demand feeds the latter. Late-chapter double- or triple-declared ambitions can supply more than half the objective in a single chapter — factor this in when deciding how hard to contest early chapters.

- Secure the Imperial Council immediately and leave it Decided — an open Council is a stolen First Regent.
- If the Council is stolen, recover it via Summit Petition (seize initiative off an Event card if needed) or Council Intrigue; losing First Regent is an objective failure.
- Enforce Policy of Escalation almost always: more Imperial ships on the board strengthens all future Acts.
- Use the Imperial Demand sparingly — enough to win ambitions, not enough to push Regents out of the Empire.
- Distribute Dealmakers "Deals" across rivals evenly; gifting one player too many cards turns them into a rival power and neuters future negotiations.
- Maintain a stockpile of Relics — Dealmakers requires a secure action, and relying on Aggression cards alone is playing with one hand tied behind your back.

**Key kit:**
- **Dealmakers** — lets you secure a Court card in a single turn whenever a rival has influence on it; the engine behind court dominance, ambition wins, and captive generation. Guard it: a failed Act 1 puts it into the court deck for any rival to claim.
- **Imperial Authority** — lets you tax any Empire-controlled city as if Loyal, giving you broader resource access than any other Fate; requires Regents to actually use Imperial ships, so foster that behaviour.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — negotiate early: help them trigger Edicts and Policy of Escalation in exchange for not contesting First Regent. Their success adds Rogue Admirals to the court deck, which you will desperately want in Acts 2 and 3; their failure adds Imperial Defectors, which directly threaten your fleet. You want the Admiral to succeed this Act.
- **Believer** — their Faithful Action Cards are extra turns for you via Dealmakers; let them teach and then negotiate shared ambition wins. Avoid taking every card every time or they will stop influencing entirely.
- **Founder / Partisan / Caretaker** — all competing for ambition wins; watch turn order and ambition declarations closely. You can usually out-manoeuvre them in the Court, but don't lose sight of the actual point total.

## Act 2 — Govern the Reach *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score 22 objective points across 3 chapters: 1 per cluster with any Empire-controlled system (while First Regent at chapter end) plus 1 per resource in the Imperial Trust (regardless of First Regent status). Empire control in all 6 clusters every chapter is worth 18 — the remaining 4+ must come from Trust resources. You cannot afford to lose First Regent in more than 1 chapter; if you lose it for 2, the Trust needs an average of 5–6 resources at every chapter end, which is very difficult.

- Aim to hold First Regent at the end of at least 2 of 3 chapters; the third can rotate if you compensate with Trust resources.
- Prioritise Imperial control of gates — catapulting is how you project Imperial force across the map, and losing gates turns a 1-action repositioning into a multi-turn slog.
- Kill Blight in gates first; impeded catapult movement costs you both this objective and Act 3.
- Feed the Trust early every chapter — it is far easier to add 1–2 resources steadily than to cram 4–5 in a final chapter.
- You do not need to win ambitions this Act; ceding them is your cheapest diplomatic currency.

**Key kit:**
- **Imperial Protectors** — Blight no longer damages ships in Imperial systems; makes the Empire visibly safer for Regents, slows Blight's erosion of your gate network, and eases system control scoring.
- **Imperial Quorum** — the updated Edict gives other Regents a stake in Empire decisions; use it to keep Regents invested rather than going Outlaw, while steering policy away from War (which stops taxing and empties the Trust).
- **Rogue Admirals** (court card, added by Admiral success) — lets you move Imperial ships independently of Loyal ships; if you can secure a copy this Act, do so immediately; you need it for Act 3.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — helpful in the short term (they hunt Outlaws and reinforce the Empire) but dangerous if they succeed; an Act 3 Admiral is nearly unwinnable for Steward. Prioritise Trust resources over planet control to widen the gap between your objectives; use Dealmakers to prevent them capturing Regent agents from the Court.
- **Pirate** — public enemy number one: their Act 2 objective centres on securing the Council as an Outlaw and stealing the Imperial Trust. Guard the Council obsessively and contest any Council fight immediately.
- **Founder** — an Outlaw whose Armistice cards can restrict your combat options; leave the Commonwealth immediately if present. Winning the Commonwealth Ambition as a non-member is a plausible windfall.
- **Planet Breaker** — do not let them destroy a Psionic planet; Outrage on Psionics strips Dealmakers. If they are a Regent, make the Imperial Demand annoying enough to nudge them toward leaving — then use Imperial ships to contain them.

## Act 3 — Imperial Reckoning *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two Grand Ambitions scored at each chapter end: (1) Imperial control of every Outlaw city (or no Outlaws remaining); (2) more resources in the Imperial Trust than the current chapter number. Grand Ambition 2 is the consistent one — aim to score it every chapter. Grand Ambition 1 is situational; delay a serious push until chapters 3–4 when the point gap between 1 and 2 Grand Ambitions is largest (25 points vs. 7 in the early chapters). In the meantime, bank strength and suppress C-Fate threats.

- Filling the Trust is manageable through court manipulation you already know; the risk is Outlaws stealing it by securing the Council — prevent this above all else.
- For Outlaw city control: Rogue Admirals is what makes this viable at distance; without it, Imperial ships reach Outlaw territory slowly, so pre-position early and strike in chapters 3–4.
- Govern with Authority Edicts now tax Outlaw city and flagship controllers too; use this to drain Outlaw resources and stack the Trust simultaneously.
- If rivals have pivoted to C-Fates, shift focus to policing them — a 100-point power lead is irrelevant if a C-Fate completes their Final Objective. Share just enough power to keep A/B Fate rivals invested in the power race.
- Sponsor yourself with Imperial Sponsor on ambitions you will declare and win; beware: anyone who declares that ambition takes a resource from the Trust.

**Key kit:**
- **Govern with Authority** — buffed Edicts that extend the Imperial Demand to Outlaws controlling Outlaw cities or flagships; directly enables both Grand Ambitions at once.
- **Imperial Sponsor** — extra tool for Trust-filling and self-sponsoring ambitions; use conservatively to avoid handing rivals a resource drain.
- **Rogue Admirals** (court card) — your only reliable answer to both Martial Law and long-range Outlaw city control; securing a copy in Act 2 is the single highest-leverage thing you can do to set up Act 3.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — Martial Law prevents you from controlling Empire-controlled systems and blocks catapult through Imperial gates; without Rogue Admirals this is near-unwinnable. If Admiral succeeded in Act 2, seriously consider pivoting Fates entirely before Act 3 rather than facing this matchup.
- **Conspirator** — favourable: Dealmakers lets you win influence battles and guess conspiracies more easily by securing cards. Guess declared ambitions with an obvious winner; avoid reckless guesses that advance them.
- **Redeemer** — destroys Relics, which guts Dealmakers; hoard Relics, deny them Relic city control, and use Edicts aggressively to drive them into negative Power via Rebuke of the Keepers.
- **Founder** — Outlaw by definition and a target for Grand Ambition 1; strike their fleet first to limit rebuild capacity. Imperial ships parked on Free cities bait them into attacking you, which you can exploit with superior numbers built over Acts 1 and 2.
- **Gate Wraith / Pathfinder / Warden / Naturalist / Guardian** — all can turn your Imperial fleet against you if they steal First Regent; keep the Council Decided and prioritise recovering it immediately if lost.

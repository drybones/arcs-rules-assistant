# Pirate — Strategy Primer

The Pirate is a Flagship Fate built around theft and aggression: your objectives demand that you take resources from Rivals and the Free states, and your ability to do so depends entirely on sustained access to battle actions. You cannot rely on the deck for Weapons — you must maintain them actively, through taxation, raiding, or the Control Array. Everything else follows from this: a healthy Flagship with a Slipstream Drive, a fleet large enough to bully smaller forces, and an opportunistic eye for the weakest pockets at the table.

---

## Act 2 — Fill the Hoard *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance your objective marker by collecting resources into the Pirate Hoard. At each chapter end you score 1 per resource in the Hoard plus 2 for each resource type where you hold more than any one Rival holds in total. Maximising the number of types you can "Embarrass Your Rivals" with slashes the raw quantity you need to steal. Aim to stack at least one type heavily in chapter 1; the Hoard persists between chapters, so an early lead compounds quickly.

- Free buildings are your most reliable target — they require only 1 key per resource and are rarely defended. Visit as many as possible over the Act.
- Roll conservatively against undamaged Free cities: 1–2 raid dice balances probability of success against Outrage risk.
- Raiding Rival buildings is a win-win: it fills the Hoard *and* depletes their resources. Prioritise weakly defended starports (destroying them never Provokes Outrage) and systems where multiple hits let you roll Skirmish and Raid dice in the same battle.
- The Imperial Council is a bonus, not a plan — grab it when turn order hands it to you cheaply and the Trust is full.
- Accept roughly 2 Outrage types over the Act, but keep Weapon Outrage off the table at all costs; without battle actions the whole strategy collapses.
- Declare Warlord late in the Act once you've already thinned Rival fleets — cash in a trophy lead when retaliation is least likely.

**Key kit:**
- **Pirate Hoard** — the central resource store; holds an unlimited number of stolen goods and can be spent, traded, or protected through Act end resolution. Don't forget the Hoard's resources are spendable during play.
- **Pirate Fleet** — lets you collect raid dice against damaged ships rather than only buildings; most useful against Flagship Fates that lack map buildings. Combine with Skirmish dice: damage ships first, then raid them.
- **Slipstream Drive** — free Prelude move every chapter; build it early and protect it with Armor. Rivals will target it first to slow you down.
- **Defense Array / Tractor Beam** — starting upgrades that deter Rivals from attacking you. Keep them online, especially Defense Array.
- **Cartels (Magnate failure cards)** — these force resource discards including from the Hoard. Secure them immediately and discard them to steal a resource; hold Relics online to grab them before a Rival does.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — leave the Commonwealth before the first Armistice or your Hoard will be empty and locked out. An experienced Founder will police Free buildings; destroy their ships near Free cities proactively.
- **Magnate** — ideal raid target: they habitually hold multiple 1-key resources. Take their stuff freely, but consider whether leaving them with nothing makes you their easiest win condition.
- **Peacekeeper** — their whole game is denying Weapons; let them operate only if they'll spare you one. Otherwise raid the Arsenal Keepers and put those Weapons on the Hoard.
- **Blight Speaker** — a scattered fleet is prime Blight food. Hit the Blight Speaker now, before Act 3, by raiding the resources they need to win ambitions; clear Blight from planets you visit.
- **Warden** — Feudal Law makes raiding a Lord from within their Fief prohibitively expensive (Outrage of all city types). Raid the Warden without your Flagship present; Vassal cities in the same Fief can be destroyed with zero Outrage — ideal targets.

## Act 3 — Protect the Haven *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** You secretly choose a Haven planet, marked by 4 Rumor tokens (2 real, 2 false) spread across the map. Grand ambitions are scored by declaring the board state of the Haven's system at chapter end; Rivals earn a power windfall if they control and identify the Haven at game end. The twin priorities are: keep the Haven's location unknown for as long as possible, and score grand ambitions when the power gain outweighs the information you reveal. Spread Rumors optimally — one per cluster, covering all three planet symbols — to maximise potential Haven locations (8 planets). Each declaration narrows the field; track what you've revealed.

- Rebuild the Hoard to at least 2 resources immediately — this is the prerequisite for any grand ambition scoring.
- Leave the Empire as early as possible; even a single fresh Imperial ship lets First Regent control any Haven system. Clear Imperial ships aggressively or guarantee you are the sole Regent.
- Maintain fresh Loyal ships in as many potential Haven systems as possible — you need coverage across clusters, which requires a large fleet and constant map presence.
- Suppress Rival fleet sizes: lone ships are your grand ambition infrastructure and can't survive against a packed Rival force.
- Control the gates. Slipstream Drive plus gate control lets your Flagship teleport anywhere each Prelude, drop ships into potential Haven systems, and threaten any Rival build-up before it becomes lethal.
- Keep 1–2 ships at the gates behind your Flagship to blunt assassination runs; proactively strike any Rival massing a large fleet at a starport.
- If a Rumor is uncovered, cut losses and declare more aggressively — 14 power in chapter 3 beats a failed 20-power attempt in chapter 4.

**Key kit:**
- **Rumors of the Haven** — the law governing your Rumor tokens and grand ambitions; understand the coordinate system (cluster + symbol = planet) before placing anything.
- **Sharing Rumors** — Rivals who learn your Haven's location have an incentive to keep it to themselves for the 10-power control bonus; use this to sow distrust between them.
- **Rumors Spread (Vox cards, ×2)** — a Rival who secures one places an agent on a Rumor; a Crisis flips it for everyone to see. Secure these yourself immediately.
- **Gatekeepers** — holds gates with a single ship against 1–2 Rival attackers; alternatively discard to place 6 ships anywhere on the map for a mid-game fleet surge.
- **Hidden Harbors (Lore)** — makes you nearly unraidable at sufficient upgrade count; Rivals cannot steal agents onto Rumors in battle.
- **Portal Seekers (Pathfinder Act 2 Failure)** — controlling the Portal at chapter end scores a grand ambition while revealing minimal information about the Haven.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — Martial Law and their planet-control grand ambition both threaten your Haven. Wage open war on Imperial ships and rally other players to do the same; no one wants Martial Law at their gates.
- **Peacekeeper** — Peace tokens can shield your Haven from raids, but a Peacekeeper monopolising Weapons locks you out of battle entirely. Deny them Weapons first; use Peace tokens as a secondary shield.
- **Gate Wraith** — The Dead Live makes ships unkillable and Twisted Passage lets Rivals reach any cluster instantly; traditional fleet suppression becomes unreliable. Double down on deception: keep more potential Haven systems viable and bluff aggressively. Your Flagship is safe from raids in the Twisted Passage if you park there.
- **Blight Speaker / Naturalist** — unchecked Blight destroys lone ships across the map, dismantling your grand ambition coverage. Clear Blight in systems you care about and contest their control.

# Magnate — Strategy Primer

The Magnate is a resource empire built in three stages: trade favours in Act 1 to establish a board presence, hoard majorities in Act 2 to lock down Monopolies, then scrap those Monopolies in Act 3 to either score Grand Ambitions or torch the resources your rivals depend on. All three acts reward having cities on every planet type, maintaining a healthy relationship with the Empire, and keeping the table friendly enough that rivals keep trading with you — right up until the moment you don't need them to.

---

## Act 1 — Trade Your Way to the Board *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 2 for each Favor or resource received during Summit Negotiations (or discard 3 resources for 2 advancement). Aim for 8 total, ideally finishing by Chapter 2 so the rest of Act 1 is spent building for Act 2. Your secondary goal is to exit Act 1 with 3+ cities spread across different planet types and majorities in at least one (ideally two) resource types.

- Secure the Imperial Council to call Summits on your schedule — it also positions you well for Acts 2 and 3.
- Sell ambitions, not just resources: declare Keeper or Tycoon, use Merchant League to gain 2 matching resources, tax more, then trade them to someone who needs to win that ambition. Any resource received advances your Objective.
- Play rivals off each other — if one player needs to lead Tycoon, sell Fuel to another to flip the lead, then wait for the first player to come to you.
- Never refuse a good-enough trade. Two resources advance your Objective by 4; almost any deal paying that is profitable.
- Keep all non-binding deals. Your word is your credit; rivals who trust you will trade more freely.
- Export (discard 3 resources for 2 advancement) only as a last resort — losing 6 resources before Act 2 hurts your Monopoly setup badly.

**Key kit:**
- **Merchant League** — gain 2 resources of any type whenever you declare; stacks across declares, unlimited. The engine that drives both your Objective and your Monopoly setup. Trade resources off it before a Crisis discards them.
- **Imperial Council** — lets you call Summits without initiative, triggers Edicts for resource collection, and sets you up as First Regent. Essential for controlling trade timing.
- **Shipping/Mining Interest** — each is essentially a free Monopoly in Act 2; secure them in Act 1 and hold both Prelude actions for then.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — support their Act 1 success; the Commonwealth makes taxing easier and cities safer from raiding, both vital for your Act 2 plan. Trade them ships if they need Free City control.
- **Admiral / Believer** — both have hard Act 1 Objectives and will pay well for what they need (starports/ships or specific cities). Anticipate the demand; trade board position only when the price is high.
- **Caretaker** — Crises discard your Merchant League resources; trade them away immediately in any game with the Caretaker. Don't spend their Golems if it helps trigger more Crises.
- **Steward** — their frequent Imperial Demands will drain you in Acts 2 and 3; take them out of First Regency early, before they become entrenched.

## Act 2 — Corner the Market *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 5/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance by gaining Monopolies from the supply ("Corner the Market" — 1 advance each) and by holding Monopolies at each chapter end ("And Hold It" — 1 per Monopoly). Target 3 Monopolies gained from supply and 3 held at each of the three chapter ends. Chapters 1 and 2 are your window to claim from the supply; rivals will deny you in Chapter 3.

- Rush the Imperial Council early in Chapter 1: lead Administration, declare (trigger Merchant League), use Psionic + Relic to secure the Council, tax for resources, collect Imperial Demand — then call Edicts and claim Monopolies immediately.
- Always take Monopolies from the supply where possible; stealing one from a rival doesn't advance your Objective and creates enemies.
- Use Monopoly Consent to block trades that would give rivals a majority — but use it sparingly; resentment is the Magnate's biggest threat.
- Shift ambition declares toward Tyrant and Warlord to draw rivals' attention away from resource hoarding when you need to protect Monopolies.
- If a rival is hoarding enough of a resource to take a Monopoly from the supply, trade 3-for-3 during the Summit before Edicts to keep it there for you.
- Don't discard Merchant League resources as freely as in Act 1 — 2 resources stored there plus 3 in slots gives you a protected majority that can't be raided.

**Key kit:**
- **Monopolies** — Fuel, Material, Weapon, Relic, Psionic cards. Holding one counts the supply resources as yours for ambitions and grants Monopoly Consent. War Profiteering (on the Weapon Monopoly) makes Weapons count for Warlord — every resource is now ambition-relevant.
- **Mining/Shipping Interest** — Prelude action fills 3 empty slots with Fuel or Material directly from supply, bypassing rivals' ability to deny you. Time it when you have 3 free slots.
- **Elder Broker** (added by Magnate) — takes resources from rivals without raiding; use it to prevent rivals building toward a majority you want.
- **Imperial Council** — holding the Imperial Trust lets you store resources Merchant-League-style and collect Imperial Demand; with careful use, secures a third batch of resources for a third Monopoly.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — the Commonwealth makes taxing flexible and grants Armistice protection against raids. Stay in it; don't steal the Commonwealth ambition unless your Objective is complete.
- **Peacekeeper** — let them have the Weapon Monopoly; it locks up Weapons on Arsenal Keepers, making it very hard for you to hold. Pursue the other four.
- **Pacifist** — expanding Psionic supply makes your Psionic Monopoly progressively harder to strip. Take it early and let the pool grow around you.
- **Pirate** — competing directly for resource hoarding and will raid when ahead; hit them first and hard before they build their bucket.
- **Steward/Admiral** — a strong Empire means Closed Economy hits rivals harder than you in Act 3; quietly support their Empire work.

## Act 3 — Monopolise or Destroy *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score both Grand Ambitions by holding 2+ Monopolies at each chapter end. At each chapter end you must scrap one Monopoly (and all matching resources in your play area). The core tension is replacing scrapped Monopolies fast enough to keep scoring — or, if rivals can deny that, scrapping resources that cripple their Grand Ambitions or Final Objectives instead.

- **Closed Economy** (you're a Regent): rivals in the Empire cannot tax Imperial-controlled cities; yours also freeze, but Merchant League and Interest cards compensate. Hold 3 resources matching each Monopoly you want to keep; scrap Monopolies where Empire ships cover fewest matching planets first.
- **Open Economy** (you're an Outlaw): rivals can tax freely too, but you can tax rival and Free cities multiple times per card. Use Prison Wardens to capture agents each time; use Prospectors to place cities on resource types you need.
- Switch economy status based on your lead: Closed is best when ahead with multiple Monopolies locked down; Open is better when behind or when you need to pivot quickly.
- When scrapping tactically: scrap Relics to stall the Redeemer; scrap Fuel/Material to hamper the Guardian; scrap Psionics to expose the Naturalist to Blight; scrap Weapons before a Peacekeeper proliferates peaceful clusters.
- Declare ambitions matching Monopolies you're hoarding — rivals can't compete if the supply is yours.

**Key kit:**
- **Closed/Open Economy** — the Act 3 law determined by your status. Choosing whether to stay Regent or go Outlaw is the single most important decision of Act 3.
- **Prospectors** (added by Magnate) — build cities on any empty slot of a resource type you don't hold the Monopoly for. Crucial for pivoting to new Monopolies after scrapping, and for abusing Open Economy taxing.
- **Prison Wardens** (added by Magnate at Act 1 end) — links tax to captive-taking; in Open Economy, taxing a rival city multiple times per turn generates both resources and captives for Tyrant.
- **Merchant League / Interest cards** — carry forward from Act 2; still the fastest way to assemble a majority after scrapping.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Redeemer** — coordinate with rivals to pool all Relics and hand them to you; scrap them immediately. Make declaring Keeper the table's payment for removing this threat.
- **Guardian** — scrap Fuel and Material Monopolies with as many resources as possible; winning Tyrant first sends their stockpile back to the supply (i.e. back to your Monopoly) for you to scrap.
- **Survivalist** — in Open Economy, control one of their cities and tax captives until they run out of agents and can't build Bunkers.
- **Admiral** — Martial Law plus Closed Economy blocks taxing of all Imperial cities for everyone including you; leave the Empire in Chapters 3–4 if you need to pivot to new Monopolies.
- **Conspirator** — scrapping resources narrows ambition outcomes and aids their manipulation; delay scrapping Relics and Psionics as long as possible, since securing Vox cards is the best Conspirator counter.
- **Advocate** — scrapping resources inflates the value of Guild cards for ambitions, undermining Closed Economy ambition declarations. Prioritise scrapping resources that match ambitions you'd otherwise dominate.

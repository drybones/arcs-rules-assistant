# Blight Speaker — Strategy Primer

The Blight Speaker enters the campaign as a B fate in Act 2, having brokered first contact with the Blight — an ancient, hungry intelligence the rest of the Reach treats as a plague. Your campaign arc is symbiotic: nurture Blight populations by spreading spores and coexisting with them to complete your Act 2 objective, then weaponise that relationship in Act 3 to unleash coordinated Crises and deny Warlord to every rival. Blight is simultaneously your shield, your engine, and your most dangerous liability.

---

## Act 2 — Nurture the Swarm *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 18/16/14 times (2/3/4 players) by placing Blight via Spore Ships and by winning ambitions while your Loyal ships coexist with Blight. Target five or six advances per chapter. Placing Blight and being present with it are deeply linked: each Spawn both advances the objective by one and converts a single fresh Blight into two damaged Blight — doubling your ambition-win advances while reducing Crisis damage. Prioritise systems with fresh Blight early; aim for stable presence in at least three systems by the end of chapter one.

- Spread ships across multiple Blight systems early, then consolidate into a fortified home base (starport + a couple of damaged Blight) once Event cards begin appearing.
- Vacate Blight systems near the start of each chapter and migrate back in as Event cards are played and the Council is decided — this timing alone can save you several ships per chapter.
- Build rather than repair after a Crisis: a fresh ship gives two health, a repair gives one.
- Prefer taxing Material planets over repairing ships where possible; use the Material for Spawn actions to bank advances and build fresh ships later.
- Stay in the Empire when viable. Policy of Escalation as First Regent places 2 Imperial ships in your systems for a single Material — cheap Crisis insurance.
- Keep the Council decided frequently; whether you hold it or an allied fate does, fewer unresolved Event cards means fewer Crises.
- A rival with Rogue Admirals or Imperial Officers can move Imperial ships away from you or destroy your Blight — watch for this and consider leaving the Empire if you cannot secure those cards yourself.

**Key kit:**
- **Spore Ships** — protected Guild card; lets you place Blight (each placement advances the objective) and ignore Blight when catapulting. The foundation of your entire Act 2 loop.
- **Mining Interest** — chain Manufacture into Spawn: accrue Material for Tycoon, then spend it via Spawn to advance the objective and grow the Blight population in one sequence.
- **Elder Broker** — the Prelude at chapter end is a tempo burst: Fuel into a fresh-Blight system for presence, then Material into Spore Ships for a final round of advances.
- **Blight Looms** — place agents on it and let it sit; the Crisis effect adds Blight to the map. Secure it only to deny rivals if many damaged Blight are already on the board.
- **Imperial Protectors** *(Steward success Act 1)* — prevents Blight Crises in Imperial systems. A single card that transforms your most dangerous vulnerability into a non-issue.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Caretaker** — negotiate for the Protector Golem; it can save your entire fleet after a Crisis. The Warrior can let rivals finish off damaged systems post-Crisis, so track where it goes.
- **Founder** — Commonwealth Armistices cover your vulnerability to opportunistic attacks while you're blight-damaged; welcome them.
- **Admiral** — their Imperial fleet presence threatens your Blight; Imperial ships can be moved away from your Blight or used to destroy it. Secure Rogue Admirals to control Imperial ships yourself, or become Outlaw and start removing Imperial ships near your systems.
- **Planet Breaker** — Psionic Outrage can strip Spore Ships; losing catapult-through-Blight mobility is a serious setback. Protect your Psionic supply and guard the card.

## Act 3 — Unleash the Fury *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Complete two Grand Ambitions — ensure no rival wins Warlord, and accumulate more pieces on Blight Hunger than the current chapter number. Both run simultaneously. The Blight Hunger ambition is fed passively by Crisis casualties; focus actions on positioning Blight to trigger large Crises and on denying rivals the Warlord ambition. Do not over-commit your own fleet to feeding Blight Hunger early; in chapters 1–2 small Crises on lone ships are sufficient, and preserving your board position lets you control rivals more effectively than chasing your own ambition.

- Early chapters: grow Blight populations via Spawn and Blight Fury (repair), keep them in gates where Crises are cheaper to weather and where Blight can be mobilised quickly later.
- Mid-game: escort fleets of 4+ fresh Blight into rival clusters, push them onto planets, then sweep in with ships to take Trophies and deny Warlord.
- Keep Warlord off the board as long as possible by leading other ambitions rivals will contest; stockpile Trophies so that when Warlord is finally declared you can win it outright.
- Spend Psionics on Blight Fury to shift the number die by one per Psionic — one Psionic raises a 30% hit chance to 50%, two Psionics to near-certainty on a target cluster.
- Sacrifice your own Blight deliberately using Spore Ships damage + Blight Hunter to rack up Trophies fast when you need to contest Warlord.

**Key kit:**
- **Blight Fury** — move and repair Blight, trigger Crises in a cluster; Psionic-augmented rolls turn low-probability wipes into reliable ones. The centrepiece of Act 3 offence.
- **Blight Hunger** — passively tracks Crisis casualties; more pieces here than the chapter number completes one Grand Ambition. Position Blight to trigger large Crises near chapter end for maximum accumulation.
- **Blight Purge** — securing this denies rivals the ability to score 2 Power per Blight Trophy via the same card; also lets you cash in your own Blight stockpile. Always compete for this.
- **Blight Hunters** — halves the hits needed to kill Blight; lets you harvest your own population for Trophies when Warlord is live. Deny it to rivals at all costs — a rival holding it can dismantle your Blight presence quickly.
- **Blight Reapers** — harvest Blight in non-critical systems for resources; pair with Spore Ships (damage) to prep Blight for easy removal. Frees up actions in cluttered systems.
- **Loyal Empaths** *(Partisan success Act 1)* — spend any resource as Psionics; turns Material and Fuel surpluses into Blight Fury shots and dramatically increases Crisis reliability.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Caretaker** — combine Blight Fury with the Warrior Golem: trigger a Crisis then immediately follow with a Warrior activation to clear survivors. Three Trophies per fresh Blight. Use the Protector to survive the same Crises you're inflicting on others.
- **Magnate** — Material and Psionics are your two key resources; contest their Monopolies or at least secure a strong share of those resources before they scrap them.
- **Admiral** — Martial Law is a serious threat if you are in the Empire without Imperial Officers or Rogue Admirals; Blight cannot move in systems held by Imperial ships you don't control. Either secure those cards or go Outlaw and strip nearby Imperial ships. Your natural ability to erase fleets gives you leverage here — use Blight to thin the Admiral's ships and Empire fleet.
- **Peacekeeper** — Ceasefire does not stop Blight Crises; use Blight Fury freely in Peaceful clusters. Expect less Warlord declaration overall, so hoard Blight Purge cards to deny rivals the scoring windfall.
- **Survivalist** — control the systems where their Bunkers are and move your Blight away; this severs their entire advance loop. Expect retaliation once they recognise what you're doing.
- **Naturalist** — you both want Blight, but they want it calm and spread; you want it concentrated and furious. Hoard Psionics to prevent them triggering Blight Society (Crisis suppression). One well-timed Crisis in a system with fresh Blight and their Loyal ships ends their chapter. Conversely, they can do the same to you — do not stack your own ships with fresh Blight unless you're confident the Crisis window is closed.

# Redeemer — Strategy Primer

The Redeemer enters Act 3 on a mission of erasure: hoard Relics, dominate the Keeper ambition, then shatter the Relics to close out the game. It is a C Fate with a Final Objective rather than Grand Ambitions, meaning it wins outright by advancing its marker to 0 — provided it has at least 1 Power. The path is narrow but one of the most efficient in the campaign when conditions are favourable; assess your starting position carefully before committing.

---

## Act 3 — Subdue and Shatter *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 16/14/12 (2/3/4 players) by two mechanisms: advance 3 whenever you win the Keeper ambition or it goes undeclared (Subdue the Keepers), and advance 2 per Relic scrapped whenever you win any ambition (Shatter the Relics). You need at least 1 Power to win. The core loop is to lead Keeper so dominantly that rivals dare not declare it, bank several "Subdue" advances across early chapters, then burst to victory by scrapping most or all Relics in a single chapter end.

- Grab Relic Fence from the court immediately — it sits on top of the deck. It is your primary Relic accumulation engine and the key to leading Keeper.
- Build all your cities as early as possible: you need resource slots to store Relics and city bonuses to offset Power drain from Rebuke of the Keepers.
- Declare ambitions other than Keeper early to place the 9/4 marker elsewhere; this reduces the Power loss if Rebuke of the Keepers triggers while you are not leading Keeper.
- Time your Relic scrapping carefully: holding Relics suppresses Keeper declarations, but scrapping in one burst is harder if you need to win another ambition first. Aim to advance 2× via Subdue the Keepers before your burst, then scrap the remainder.
- Edicts are dangerous — contest the Imperial Council to prevent voluntary Edict declarations; becoming First Regent is viable if it lets you freeze Edict timing.
- If a rival leads Keeper and triggers Rebuke of the Keepers repeatedly, pivot to winning other ambitions (Warlord if clearing cities, Tyrant if controlling rival Relic cities) to claw back Power and reposition.

**Key kit:**

- **Ire of the Keepers (Lore)** — prevents you from declaring Keeper or taxing Loyal Relic cities you don't control. Rivals will use it to deny Relic access; you must control Relic cities (yours and rivals') to work around it. Controlling rival Relic cities also yields captives.
- **Rebuke of the Keepers (Edict)** — the Redeemer's biggest liability. Each Edict while you are not leading Keeper drains Power equal to what Keeper's winner would score. Lead Keeper or suppress Edicts to prevent a death spiral.
- **Relic Fence (Guild)** — placed on top of the court deck at setup, so it is available in chapter 1. Lets you accumulate Relics regardless of board position. Secure it fast; if rivals steal it repeatedly, consider provoking Relic Outrage to discard it from play rather than letting it be turned against you.

**Notable matchups:**

- **Magnate** — the most dangerous rival. Monopolies and the closed economy can strip Relics from the map entirely; a closed economy blocks taxing Empire-controlled cities. Only pursue Redeemer into this matchup if you can contest the Relic monopoly early.
- **Warden** — Feudal Law lets them claim Relic-rich clusters as Fiefs, hoarding Relics and contesting Keeper while also threatening Rebuke of the Keepers triggers. Oppressive matchup; consider avoiding Redeemer if Warden is in play.
- **Founder** — the Commonwealth lets every member tax Free Relic cities, multiplying rival Relic access. Stay in the Commonwealth if possible; leaving is a move of desperation. Winning Keeper as the Commonwealth ambition can recover significant lost Power.
- **Caretaker** — generally favourable lore additions, but the extra Event card means roughly two additional Edict opportunities in Act 3. Manage your Keeper lead accordingly.
- **Planet Breaker** — destroying Relic planets cuts your access and can wipe your stored Relics at chapter end. The saving grace: Lostworld Refugees (added if Planet Breaker is present) clears Relic Outrage and refills your Relic slots when you help refugees resettle.

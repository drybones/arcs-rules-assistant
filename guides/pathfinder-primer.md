# Pathfinder — Strategy Primer

The Pathfinder is a B fate built around a single flagship and a clear, self-contained quest: find a hidden portal and then hold it. Both acts reward mobility over board presence — Act 2 is a solo puzzle that almost plays itself, while Act 3 is a concentrated defensive fight around the one system that matters. There is no necessary Act 1 groundwork, making it the ideal pivot for any player whose Act 1 went sideways.

---

## Act 2 — The Pilgrimage Begins *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 0/5)*

**Objective focus:** Find the portal before Act 2 ends. The player to your right secretly records a planet by cluster number and ID symbol; you search planets with your flagship using Uncovering Clues until you hit the Portal token. The objective completes immediately on discovery — you do not need to wait for chapter end.

- Guess as often as possible: each guess on a planet with buildings rewards you regardless of outcome (resources per city, ships per starport). Treat it as a prelude income stream, not just a search mechanism.
- A Check (✓) clue means the correct cluster or the correct ID symbol — take the 50/50 and guess again in the same cluster. A Cross (✗) means neither; move to a different cluster and ID.
- Delaying the final discovery is sometimes correct: once the portal is revealed you lose Uncovering Clues permanently, so if there are profitable planets left, farm them first.
- Do not deliberately fail. Failure adds Portal Seekers to the court deck — a third Grand Ambition for A and B fates that you will likely never score as a C fate pivot.
- After finding the portal, shift focus to flagship upgrades and consolidating ships around the portal cluster for Act 3.

**Key kit:**
- **Slipstream Drive** — your primary mobility tool; armor it as soon as possible (Clusters 2 and 5 have both Psionic and Relic planets if you need the upgrade resource). Everything breaks down if this is destroyed.
- **Uncovering Clues** — the core search action; requires spending a resource that does not match any planet type in the current cluster. Start in a cluster with a Psionic planet and aim for a Control Array so you can tax gate clusters to fund guesses.
- **Sprinter Drives / Loyal Pilots** — no-cost or any-resource movement; lets you reach planets you'd otherwise be locked out of. Sprinter Drives cannot be stolen; prioritize them over Loyal Pilots.
- **Rogue Admirals** — lets you reposition Imperial ships for mobility cover and, critically, clear them from the portal planet in Act 3. Secure both copies if available.
- **Loyal cards (Partisan Act 1 success)** — any Loyal card lets you spend any resource as its suit, dramatically loosening the resource constraint on Uncovering Clues.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder / Warden / Believer / Admiral / Magnate** — all favorable; their infrastructure fills the board with cities and starports you can exploit for clue rewards. Admiral in particular spawns extra starports — lean on those for free ships.
- **Blight Speaker** — contests gate clusters (they want them empty for Blight spawn setup; you need them to move freely). Strike Blight when it obstructs your path; your mobility advantage lets you pick the engagements.
- **Pirate** — the one Act 2 threat: they can strip resources you need for Uncovering Clues. Keep your fleet grouped to deter raids. If blocked, damage their flagship — your clue-farming ships give you a numbers advantage.

## Act 3 — Hold the Portal *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Two Grand Ambitions, both centered on the portal system. First: move at least 5 of 6 Pilgrims through the portal (Pilgrims can only move during the Prelude into regions you control or where your flagship is present). Second: control the portal planet at every chapter end. Both require owning the portal cluster; prioritize consolidating there from the first action of Act 3.

- Aim to move at least 2 Pilgrims through in Chapter 1. Moving all 5 in one chapter prevents a third copy of Seek the Portal entering the deck — worth attempting if the table allows it.
- Spread ships across the portal cluster rather than stacking them all on the portal planet; a single coordinated strike can wipe a concentrated force. Keep the majority on the portal planet with your flagship at the gate.
- Failing Chapter 1's Pilgrim ambition is recoverable but splits your attention in Chapter 2 and adds another Seek the Portal to the deck.
- From Chapter 2 onward, your entire game lives in this cluster. Use that focus as a diplomatic argument: point out how little you threaten others' multi-cluster objectives.

**Key kit:**
- **Pilgrims** — spawn in every cluster at Act 3 start; move only during the Prelude into regions you control or where your flagship is present. Identify the furthest Pilgrims before Act 3 and plan routes early; your flagship must cover the stragglers.
- **Seek the Portal** — always enters the court deck at game start (and again each time you move Pilgrims through the portal). Secure and remove it before opponents can exploit it: a rival who controls the portal system can scrap your entire fleet with it at zero combat cost. Use it as a deterrent by staging agents on it without triggering — but only if your portal fleet is large enough to make that threat credible.
- **Force Beams (Lore card)** — pushes ships out of the portal system without combat; counters most rival entry attempts and deters attacks by its mere presence. Avoid holding the initiative marker at chapter end if you hold this, as you may need to react to an incoming fleet.
- **Mirror Plating (Lore card)** — with a large portal fleet, makes direct combat a multi-chapter commitment for rivals; strong passive deterrent.
- **Navy Reprisal (Steward Act 2 success)** — hits all Imperial ships in a cluster; critical for clearing the Empire liability that lets rivals exploit Seek the Portal without fighting you directly.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — the hardest matchup. Their fleet rivals yours in size and Martial Law can block your movement and Empire ship access. Expect to go Outlaw against a strong Admiral; keep your flagship upgraded for solo combat and avoid building a fleet large enough to provoke early conflict.
- **Steward** — controls First Regent and can funnel Imperial ships into your portal system to enable Seek the Portal abuse without direct attack. Counter via Imperial Quorum (maximize starports on your flagship to block their Imperial action) and use Seek the Portal as a deterrent to make Empire ship incursions costly.
- **Naturalist / Blight Speaker** — Blight on cluster gates and the portal cluster obstructs Pilgrim movement. Clear gates and the portal cluster first; ignore Blight elsewhere and let rivals handle the rest of the Reach.
- **Peacekeeper / Founder** — generally favorable, though both restrict your ability to police the portal when the Empire is targeting you. Secure Armistice to manage Founder; keep a Weapon resource available against Peacekeeper. Consider leaving the Commonwealth early if they coordinate with the Empire.
- **Warden** — minor threat unless the portal falls inside a Fief, in which case Feast Day can lock you out of combat in your own cluster. Treat like Founder: be proactive about removing the relevant court card. Coexist if possible; dismantle the Fief only if necessary.

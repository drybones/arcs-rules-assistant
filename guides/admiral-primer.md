# Admiral — Strategy Primer

The Admiral's entire campaign arc runs through the Empire: build the Imperial fleet in Act 1, weaponise it against Outlaws and Regents in Act 2, then use Martial Law to lock rivals out of everything they've been sharing in Act 3. All three acts reward holding First Regent, maintaining a Weapons-heavy toolkit, and keeping both Imperial and Loyal fleets healthy on the board.

---

## Act 1 — Build the War Machine *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 5/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 13 times by placing Imperial ships during Edicts while leading an ambition, and by taking Trophies with Imperial ships. These two conditions must overlap — control the Imperial Council to time Edicts when you're already winning Warlord or Tycoon. Aim for Warlord first; Tycoon is a solid secondary if you've settled on Material/Fuel planets.

- Use Imperial Foundries **only** when leading an ambition — depleting the supply otherwise wastes your best advance mechanism.
- Drive rivals toward leaving the Empire: reposition Imperial ships away from them and trigger Imperial Demands to drain their resources. Outlaw pieces are easier Trophies than Blight.
- Prioritise Fuel, Material, and Weapon planets for your buildings; these sustain fleet production and Trophy hunting through the campaign.

**Key kit:**
- **Imperial Officers** — lets you act with Imperial ships without needing Loyal pieces present; the foundation of all fleet play.
- **Imperial Foundries** — your primary source of new Imperial ships; useless without a Loyal starport and an ambition lead, so build both early.
- **First Regent** — controls Edict timing and the Imperial Trust; hold it to lead ambitions via the Trust's resources.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — negotiate early: let them hold First Regent in exchange for enforcing Policy of Escalation and not contesting Warlord. Their success adds Hunter Squads (Weapon synergy) and prevents Empire Falls — both strongly in your interest.
- **Founder** — must go Outlaw to win, making them a consistent Trophy source throughout the campaign. Factor their starting location into setup; consider taking a Weapon planet nearby.

## Act 2 — Enforce the Empire *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 30 times — the highest requirement in the game. Sources: 2 per Outlaw Trophy, 2 per captured Regent agent, 1 per Empire-controlled planet at each chapter end. Outlaws are the most efficient source; war parties of 6+ ships hunting them is the core loop. Planet control compounds every chapter, so expand the Imperial footprint in parallel.

- Spread Imperial ships one per planet — cheap to place, expensive for rivals to dislodge.
- Don't sacrifice your entire Loyal fleet to Imperial Foundries; you need Loyal ships on the board to enter Act 3 viably.
- Leave the Commonwealth immediately if the Founder is present — one Skirmish die against a Free city removes you without Provoking Outrage.

**Key kit:**
- **Honor Guard** — protects your Weapon Guild cards while you hold any Weapon resource; everything else in your kit depends on this staying intact.
- **Rogue Admirals** — lets you ignore the Truce Law, tax Regent cities with Imperial ships for captives, and place 3 Imperial ships via Prelude. Secure both copies; a rival with one can move Imperial ships off your planets.
- **Court Enforcers** — strips agents from court cards where your Weapon count exceeds rivals'; punishes Court-dependent fates and advances your objective via captives.
- **Command Chaos** — if you lack a Rogue Admiral, secure this immediately; a rival who steals Imperial Officers through it is near game-ending.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Founder** — their objective directly blocks your planet control; leave the Commonwealth first action and hunt them. Armistice locks you out of retaliation while they can still kill Imperial ships.
- **Steward** — the alliance has soured; their success in Act 2 is now against your interests. Expect a late-chapter First Regent theft; counter by stealing it back close to chapter end yourself.
- **Partisan** — a failed Act 1 adds Outrage Spreads; one triggered Weapons Outrage can strip Imperial Officers, Honor Guard, Rogue Admirals, and Court Enforcers simultaneously.
- **Peacekeeper** — competes directly for Weapon resources and loves enforcing Policy of Escalation as First Regent; keep Weapons out of their hands and the title away from them.
- **Warden** — Feudal Law can expose you to triple-resource Outrage across many clusters. Counter: hold their Claims of Nobility Lore card to stall Fief expansion.

## Act 3 — Loyal Fleet, Loyal Victory *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Control 9 planets and hold more Loyal ships than all rivals at chapter end. Hold First Regent to count Empire-controlled planets toward your 9; if you can't, move Imperial ships away from planets you want to hold with Loyal pieces — Martial Law makes First Regent valuable enough to fight for.

- The gap planets between clusters 5/6 and 2/3 give four well-positioned planets that are efficient to fortify and recover if contested — a near-complete half of your quota.
- Use Imperial ships in the gates to block catapult movement; this slows rivals while your Loyal fleet consolidates on planets.
- Declare Warlord frequently to recycle destroyed Loyal and Imperial ships back into their supplies.

**Key kit:**
- **Martial Law** — rivals without Imperial Officers or Rogue Admirals lose control of all Empire-held systems and lose catapult access through Imperial-held gates. Secure it immediately; deny it to the Steward above all.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — Martial Law is a near-checkmate: they cannot control Empire-held systems without Imperial Officers or Rogue Admirals. Deny them both; use Rogue Admirals' Prelude to discard copies you hold rather than leave them as raid targets.
- **Founder** — still contesting planet control (they need 7, you need 9) and now has tools to destroy Imperial ships more efficiently. Treat them as the primary target.
- **Hegemon** — Happy Hosts captures any ships damaged in their controlled systems; their Banners let them keep captives indefinitely. Destroy Banners early before the snowball starts.
- **Blight Speaker / Naturalist** — unchecked Blight destroys the ships you need for both Grand Ambitions. Use Blight-clearing court cards (added after Blight Speaker's Act 2 success) and contest their system control with Imperial ships via Martial Law.

# Gate Wraith — Strategy Primer

The Gate Wraith is the Fate that rewrites the map. By catapulting into gates to destroy them and replace them with Passage tokens, you progressively dismantle the board's infrastructure while opening the Twisted Passage — a new system you must control to advance. Everything about the Gate Wraith rewards tempo and aggression: break gates early to multiply your per-ambition scoring, spend the rest of the campaign winning ambitions and controlling the Passage in a burst at each chapter's end. The game you end up playing looks nothing like the one your Rivals signed up for.

---

## Act 3 — Engine at Full Burn *(Complexity 5/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 24/21/18 times (2/3/4 players) by winning declared ambitions and controlling the Twisted Passage — each scores once per Passage token currently on the map. Scoring is non-linear: each additional broken gate multiplies every future ambition win and Passage control. The engine works in two overlapping phases:

- **Break gates first.** Aim for at least 3–4 broken gates (total) by chapter 2. Each gate broken now is worth more in chapters 3–4 than it will ever be again. Use Catapult Overdrive to commandeer Rival starports and break gates in one move; your own Loyal starports work too.
- **Transition to scoring.** Once 4–5 gates are broken, stop chasing new ones. Use your remaining actions to declare and win ambitions and to flood the Twisted Passage at end-of-chapter. Late-chapter burst is real — even with half your objective left, two scoring events in chapter 4 can close it out.
- Declare ambitions that align with what you are doing anyway: **Tycoon** early (you need Fuel to break gates), **Warlord** only if you already lead on Trophies, **Empath** if you are hoarding Psionics for extra raid actions.
- Keep your fleet distributed across the map during chapters, not parked in the Passage. Move in late to contest control — Passage Storms triggers on every Crisis and will shred ships sitting there all chapter.
- To control the Passage at chapter end: save a high-pip Mobilization or Aggression card as your last card. If Imperial ships are there and you are an Outlaw, destroy them; if you are a Regent but not First Regent, race to act last.
- Prefer going Outlaw — Imperial ships in the Passage are a constant threat to your control, and as an Outlaw you can simply destroy them. First Regent is the only reason to stay a Regent, and only if no one is fighting you for it.
- Construction and Material are nearly useless after early gate-breaking; The Dead Live returns all ships after each chapter, so your fleet rarely needs rebuilding. Save Construction cards to copy; never hoard Material.
- Raid Rivals' Fuel and Psionics to make contesting the Passage expensive for them — they need movement resources to get there just as you do.

**Key kit:**

- **Catapult Overdrive** — catapult from Rival starports to break gates in one action; enormously efficient in early chapters before Rivals can react. Value falls off once most gates are gone.
- **Breaking Gates / Placing Passages** — the engine itself; each token placed multiplies every future advance. Prioritise gates in clusters with free building slots or far from your position (harder to reclaim) when choosing where to start.
- **The Dead Live** — ships return to the Twisted Passage after each chapter, removing the normal cost of aggressive raiding. You can take riskier battles knowing your fleet comes back; rivals can too, so treat buildings as a liability and destroy your own starports as you use them.
- **Passage Storms (Vox)** — not yours, but always in the court and always dangerous. Weaponise it: keep your fleet out of the Passage until the last moment, and time your entry after the final Crisis of the chapter. Can be used offensively at chapter start when all ships (including Rivals') return to the Passage from The Dead Live.
- **Elder Broker** — grants Material, Fuel, and Weapon in one card; the gate-breaking starter kit. Grab it early.
- **Living Structures** — converts your cities to starports (more gate-breaking platforms) and eliminates them as raid targets simultaneously.
- **Empath's Vision** — reroll the event die when leaving the Passage (requires Empath declared); lets you control where you and Rivals can move out of it.
- **Song of Freedom** — when secured, shuffles the deck, temporarily burying Passage Storms; also removes unwanted cities from the map.
- **Union Cards** — give you an extra card, effectively an extra end-of-chapter action; pick Aggression for raiding flexibility or Mobilization for maximum Passage-control pips.
- **Shipping Interest** — discard for a flood of Fuel early on; also enables easy Tycoon wins.
- **Rogue Admirals** *(if Admiral succeeded Act 1)* — move Imperial ships out of the Passage without spending your own actions; break gates anywhere on the map trivially; ignore Truce Law to destroy Imperial ships contesting your Passage.

**Notable matchups:**

- **Admiral** — hardest matchup. Competes for First Regent, contests Imperial ship control, and Martial Law restricts your map movement. Go Outlaw to retain the ability to destroy Imperial ships in the Passage and escape Edict taxation. Expect them to move Imperial ships into the Twisted Passage deliberately to deny you control.
- **Judge** — directly counters your win condition: ties on ambitions do not count as wins for you, and the Judge actively engineers ties. Use the threat of a Judge power-win to coax "A"/"B" Fate players into letting you take an ambition uncontested.
- **Pathfinder** — has a concrete reason to hold the Passage (Pilgrims reaching the Portal), not just a tactical one. Let them move Pilgrims through quickly if possible; they will leave once done. If not, Passage Storms is your best tool against them.
- **Caretaker** — mostly positive. Golems give you extra actions for Passage control and ambitions; the Seeker Golem lets you catapult from any system without a starport. Stone Speakers can weaponise Passage Storms — coordinate if possible. The extra Event card increases Crisis frequency, which you can exploit at chapter start when all ships are in the Passage.
- **Founder** — Armistice prevents you from harming buildings while in effect if you are a Commonwealth Member. Break gates aggressively and go Outlaw to avoid this restriction.
- **Peacekeeper** — Peace clusters block your ability to raid for resources. Keep at least one Weapon so you can flip clusters to War when you need to raid; alternatively, use Peace clusters as safe harbours to rest your fleet between scoring bursts.
- **Hegemon** — Banners let you reach distant gates faster at chapter start; the Tax-for-2-resources ability accelerates ambition setup. A net positive interaction.
- **Advocate** — you are a problem for them: Passage Storms being a Vox card constantly present in the court makes their guild-card grand ambition extremely difficult. Expect them to abandon Court strategy and play aggressively on the map — a capable force once free of Outrage pressure.

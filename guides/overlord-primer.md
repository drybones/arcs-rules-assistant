# Overlord — Strategy Primer

The Overlord wins by controlling planets and holding resources that match their active outrages — simple on paper, but execution demands a careful read of the table. Every outrage type you commit to is a liability: **Reach Rejects Overlord** drains your Power track each time an Edict fires, so the game punishes overreach as harshly as it rewards board presence. Your core tension is provoking enough outrage to advance your objective while keeping your Power bar healthy enough to survive to chapter end. Play the slow-burn tyrant — consolidate quietly, then expand when rivals are committed elsewhere.

---

## Act 3 — Rule by Fear *(Complexity 1/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 20 spaces total across **Rule by Fear Alone** (+1 per outraged resource *type* you hold) and **Control by Fear Alone** (+2 per outraged planet *type* you control) — scored at each chapter end. Types, not quantities, count, so each outrage gives you a maximum of 3 advances per chapter. Aim for 2 outrage types to start; the math works if you can score both objectives on both types for two chapters, then expand to 3–4 types for a final push.

- Target **Material and Relic** as your first two outrages — least useful to your engine, so their loss costs little.
- Avoid outraging **Weapons** early; you need a Weapon in hand to destroy Free Cities and cities on your own schedule, not just when you play Aggression cards.
- Aim for 4–6 advances in chapters 1 and 2, leaving 8–12 to close out in chapter 3 or 4 with a wider board push.
- Save Movement resources and cards for end-of-chapter maneuvering — a last-turn swing to pick up additional planet types is often the difference between 3 and 5 advances.
- **First Regent** is your preferred status: Empire Rule Law lets Imperial ships count as your control, and the Imperial Trust shelters outraged resources from Outlaw raids. If the First Regency is too contested, go **Outlaw** rather than stay a lesser Regent — Edicts tax your resources on top of hitting your Power track.
- Keep the **Imperial Council on its "Decided" side** as much as possible. When it flips to "In Session", secure it immediately and call Crises, not Edicts.
- Start "tall" (fortified positions in 1–2 clusters, ideally clusters 1 and 3 for their Material/Fuel/Weapon mix) and pivot "wide" once rivals are stretched thin. Going wide too early telegraphs your threat level.
- Trigger **Sycophants** on already-outraged resources rather than provoking new types; you get ships on the planets you need without expanding your Edict exposure.
- Win **Warlord** regularly to rebuild your Power cushion. Sycophants gives you the ships to sustain aggression; Free Cities are the ideal target (no counter-attack, instant outrage, open planet slot).

**Key kit:**
- **Sycophants** — places ships whenever you provoke outrage, directly onto the planet types you need to control. Your primary ship-building engine; triggers even on repeat outrages of the same type, so blowing up cities is more efficient than building.
- **Reach Rejects Overlord** — your built-in constraint. Lose Power equal to your outrage count each time an Edict fires. In a 3-player game expect ~9–12 Power lost to Events alone with 2 outrages; plan accordingly. Winning ambitions to rebuild your Power bar is mandatory, not optional.

**Notable Court cards to watch:**
- **Council Intrigue** (Vox) — flips the Imperial Council to In Session; secure it immediately to prevent rivals from triggering extra Edicts.
- **Partisan Seizing** (Law, if Partisan is in the game) — lets you provoke outrage by seizing initiative, gaining 2 free ships from Sycophants without risking ships in battle.
- **Outrage Spreads** (Vox, Partisan Act 1 failure) — forces all players to provoke the same outrage type; great for picking up a new type cheaply and spreading the pain.
- **The Loyal Cards** (Guild, Partisan Act 1 success) — resources not discarded on outrage; lets you spend down to one of a type without losing your objective score.
- **Council Insiders** (Guild, Steward Act 2 success) — rivals can flip the Council to In Session whenever they secure a Guild card; dangerous, prioritise denying it.
- **The Cartel Cards** (Guild, Magnate Act 1 failure) — resources held under a Cartel are discarded after scoring and do not count for Rule by Fear Alone even if you hold the card; watch for these if a Magnate rival was present.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Admiral** — worst possible matchup. Martial Law strips any benefit from Imperial ships in your systems, and you do not control Imperial ships for your objective even as First Regent. If an Act 3 Admiral is present, leave the Empire (or be Outlaw already), clear Imperial ships from your planets, and focus on contesting the Imperial Council to limit Edict frequency.
- **Steward** — they contest the First Regency and Imperial Council directly and are incentivised to call Edicts. Only take Overlord into Steward if you enter Act 3 with a substantial Power cushion (15+); expect to lose more than average to Reach Rejects Overlord.
- **Pacifist** — Rebuke of the Empaths is a hard counter: it strips resources matching your outrage types and is itself an Edict. Reconcile with the Outraged can also remove your outrage at Summits. Avoid Overlord if a Pacifist may appear.
- **Founder** — the Commonwealth limits your aggression against Free Cities (your cheapest outrage source). Leave the Commonwealth immediately and watch for Spirit of Freedom if the Founder contests the Imperial Council.
- **Magnate** — Closed Economy blocks stockpiling outraged resources for Rule by Fear Alone; Open Economy helps you tax them back. Adapt based on which side they're playing.
- **Partisan** — Terror Strike's crisis effect hits harder the more outrages you have; Sower of Division can add outrage without the Sycophants ships bonus. Still a tolerable matchup because Partisan Seizing benefits you too, and they're unlikely to target you while Edicts remain low.
- **Warden** — good matchup. Fiefs insulate your planets from aggression; Feudal Law lets you provoke outrage three times by harming a single Vassal; "Lords Gain Power" Edict is a shared incentive to keep other Edicts suppressed.
- **Planet Breaker** — favourable. Planet Hammer provoking outrage hands you free Sycophants ships; a broken city permanently frees up resource slots without ongoing raid vulnerability.
- **Redeemer** — useful meat-shield. Their burst objective draws more stopping-power from the table; they also want to suppress Edicts due to Rebuke of the Keepers. Declare Keeper to align interests.
- **Guardian** — Edenguard ambition drives rivals to contest Material and Fuel planets; if those are your outrage types, consider pivoting to Weapons, Relics, and Psionics to avoid the traffic.
- **Pirate** — extra incentive to raid your resources is painful, but The Haven offers an emergency Power refill if Edicts have been hammering you.
- **Peacekeeper** — Ceasefire Tokens force a Weapon spend to harm anything, slowing outrage provocation; offset this with a "wide" board position and lean into Power Dividends if you hold First Regent under Peace Policy.

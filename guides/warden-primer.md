# Warden — Strategy Primer

The Warden builds a feudal political order across the Reach, converting clusters into Fiefs where they are Lord and rivals become Vassals. Their power comes not from ships or ambitions but from the systemic leverage of Outrage deterrence, captive-generating taxation, and — in Act 3 — the raw Power output of repeated Edicts. The fate rewards patience and tabletalk: you are rarely the most threatening player on the board until suddenly you are, and rivals who didn't take you seriously will find the Feudal machine is already running.

---

## Act 2 — Build the Feudal Order *(Complexity 5/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 22 times by scoring *Lord Over the Fiefs* (once per city in clusters where you have a Seat, per chapter) and *Control the Fief's Seats* (up to 2 per Seat you control). Lord Over the Fiefs scales with city count, so target densely populated clusters. Aim for 3 Fiefs by the end of Chapter 2 — one Seat per chapter compounds into a reliable total; a fourth Fief is a bonus, not a requirement.

- Use the Claims of Nobility replacement Tax action to found Fiefs; attach it immediately to a court card you already have agents on (or are about to influence) so you can recover it quickly.
- Secure Claims of Nobility back as soon as possible after founding a Fief — leaving it in the court risks rivals holding it to deny you, which stalls your entire objective.
- If a rival holds Claims to stall you, cause a Crisis by securing the Imperial Council to force the card into play.
- Don't declare Warlord and avoid instigating battles against Vassals — city destruction dissolves Fiefs, and the multi-Outrage deterrent is your primary defensive tool.
- Build ships near Seat starports to control those systems and advance through Control the Fief's Seats; also gives you a fleet to threaten non-Vassal aggressors.
- Stay in the Empire if possible: Imperial ships protect Seats from Outlaws, and First Regent lets you count Empire-controlled systems toward Control the Fief's Seats.
- Plan your Fief locations deliberately: one Seat with a starport for ships, one where only you have cities (for Act 3), one with a single rival's city (manageable Vassal), and one with a city of every rival (your deterrence anchor).
- Avoid taking the Power lead — Song of Freedom triggers on the leading player and removes Seats.

**Key kit:**
- **Claims of Nobility** — the only way to found Fiefs; your entire Act 2 objective flows through it. Protect it by attaching it to court cards you influence immediately; recover it before the Act ends or it gets shuffled into the deck.
- **Seats and Fiefs law** — grants Lord taxation over every city in the cluster (one tax per city per turn) regardless of control, and makes Vassals face multi-suit Outrage for harming you. The deterrence is only as strong as the Guild cards in your Fief; establish Seats where rivals have protected Guild cards they cannot afford to lose.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Partisan** — likely already has 3+ outrages by Act 2, so the violence deterrent works less on them. Establish Seats in clusters with resources the Partisan hasn't yet outraged (especially Psionics). If table-wide outrage stacks too high by Act 2 end, consider pivoting away from Warden for Act 3.
- **Caretaker** — will actively target Claims of Nobility for its Lore value. Use the Lore-trading ability in your favour: negotiate Claims back when they want something from you, but don't sacrifice existing Seats to do it.
- **Pirate** — can destroy Vassal cities in Fiefs without Outrage, then finish the Seat with only one Outrage. Hit their flagship before they chain this together; target them while they're dismantling Vassal cities and before they reach your Seat.
- **Founder** — weakens your taxing advantage via the Commonwealth. Stay in the Commonwealth yourself to benefit from it and to position for Act 3's First Regent requirements.
- **Flagship players generally** — flagships can leave Fiefs freely and have usually already accumulated Outrage elsewhere. Hit flagships hard whenever they are outside a Fief, and keep gates controlled to reduce their speed.

## Act 3 — Edict Engine *(Complexity 5/5, Difficulty 4/5)*

**Objective focus:** Score both Grand Ambitions (*More Seats than any Rival* and *Control 4+ Seats*) primarily in Chapters 1–2 while Fiefs are intact. Do not rely on Grand Ambitions for late-chapter Power — rivals will demolish Seats once they see your Edict gains. Instead, use *Lords Gain Power* (3 Power per Fief per Edict) as your primary scoring engine, targeting a 30+ Power lead over rivals before the endgame crunch.

- Lock Feast Day in the court using a Feudal Courts token on a card in a Fief where you are Lord with no Vassals — this prevents all rivals from harming anyone in any Fief, and cannot be ransacked while Feast Day is active.
- Spam Edicts: every Edict generates 3 Power per Seat you hold as Lord. With 3+ Seats this outpaces what most fates can gain from ambitions in a chapter.
- Target at least 3 more Seats than the next rival; with that lead, Lords Gain Power is self-sustaining even if some Seats are eventually lost.
- Be First Regent under Policy of Peace: adds 5 Power per Edict (one per city) on top of your Lord bonus — makes the Power lead unassailable.
- Once the table becomes complacent under Feast Day, you can end it yourself (secure Feast Day) to destroy rival fleets before they can threaten your Seats — it gets buried rather than scrapped, so may recur.
- Counter the Feast ending: park ships on 2–3 key Seats and hold their gates; if Feast Day falls you can still protect the Fiefs that matter most.
- Maintain competing in Tyrant and resource ambitions — Lord taxation over many cities makes you the strongest Tyrant competitor throughout Act 3.

**Key kit:**
- **Lords Gain Power** — your primary Power source. 3 Power per Fief per Edict; with 3 Fiefs and 6 Edicts across a chapter, that is 54 Power from this alone. Protect it by protecting your Seats.
- **Feudal Courts** — reserves court cards for Fiefs you're Lord of; combined with Feast Day creates an impenetrable lock. Also lets you Influence or Secure during Edicts as Lord, letting you outpace Vassals in agent bidding wars.
- **Feast Day** — prevents all harm in all Fiefs; locked with a Feudal Courts token on a Vassal-free Fief, it cannot be ransacked or influenced away. Your shell. Secure it yourself when the timing is right.
- **Warden's Levy** — limited utility overall, but use it to pre-load ships before ending Feast Day, or in later chapters when violence increases. Also slightly depletes rivals' ship supplies.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Caretaker** — Golem Worship can force you to lose cities (and thus Seats). Keep resource slots full so rivals cannot dump Golems on you unexpectedly. The Caretaker can also cause Crises outside of Events, threatening Feast Day without warning.
- **Founder** — Armistice can supplement your Feast Day shell; lock it with a Feudal Courts token. Note: rivals outside the Commonwealth are unaffected, but the Founder must remain in it.
- **Flagship players** — flagships can move into a Seat where you're the only player and convert it into a Vassal Fief, destroying your ability to lock Feudal Courts on that card exclusively. Warden's Levy is more valuable in these matchups; consider levying more aggressively to limit their ship supply.
- **Overlord / Admiral** — ship-heavy fates make Warden's Levy more meaningful. Use it as a drain on their supply rather than as a direct combat tool.
- **Peacekeeper** — Ceasefire adds another protection layer; Peace Dividends can enhance your Edict power but may also narrow your Power lead over Peacekeeper. Watch their system control and levy ships from them if they start approaching your per-Edict gains.

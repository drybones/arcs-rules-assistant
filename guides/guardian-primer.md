# Guardian — Strategy Primer

The Guardian enters Act 3 as a hoarder and a brawler. Your twin levers — filling the *Green Vault* with frozen Material and Fuel for Tyrant, and controlling the eight Fuel/Material planets for Edenguard — give you two independent scoring paths, but both require constant board presence and court aggression to defend. Rivals will read your objective quickly and either declare Tyrant every chapter to drain your Captives box, or park ships on your key planets. Flexibility is your strength; knowing which path to lean on turn-by-turn is the skill.

---

## Act 3 — Guard the Reach *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 16 or 18 spaces by chapter end, scoring via two ambitions: **Tyrant** (Material/Fuel resources in your Captives box count double) and **Edenguard** (control 5 of the 8 Fuel/Material planets). You need roughly six advancements per chapter to win before Chapter 4; falling behind early is recoverable, but letting the game reach Chapter 4 risks losing the Power race.

- Keep both paths open for as long as possible — Rivals will choose one to shut down, and you pivot hard to the other.
- Prefer Tyrant and Edenguard go **undeclared**: you advance for both at chapter end with no contest. Declare other ambitions to crowd out the markers.
- If Tyrant is declared every chapter, lean on Edenguard for your steady stream of advancements and dominate the Court to keep Captives flowing.
- Target **5 of the 8** Fuel/Material planets for Edenguard. Clusters 4 (Relic planet) and 1 (Weapon planet) are the most valuable; control adjacent clusters (6+1 or 3+4) to reduce overextension.
- Act last in the chapter where possible — Rivals cannot use Fuel to dislodge you from Edenguard control without your ability to respond.
- Keep at least one Weapon in supply to enable ransacking; ransacking the Court suppresses Rival Captive generation and protects your Tyrant lead.
- Trigger Fuel and Material Outrage freely — your *Green Vault* resources are frozen and untouchable, so these Outrages cost you almost nothing.
- **Empire interaction:** If First Regent is held by a rival, the Empire blocks Edenguard entirely and can drain your *Green Vault* via Policy of Escalation/War. Either fight for First Regent or push to weaken the Empire. If outlawing, time a single large heist of the Imperial Trust (loaded with Material/Fuel from Escalation) — you can theoretically advance your full objective in one chapter this way.
- **Flagship:** Keep it only if you can grab upgrades fast without losing tempo. Re-settle on Fuel/Material planets in Clusters 1/3/4 if re-settling gets you better board position from the start.

**Key kit:**

- **Green Vault** (Lore) — the engine. Takes Material/Fuel as frozen Captives (un-raidable, count double for Tyrant and Tycoon). Freezing resources also makes Tycoon unattractive for Rivals to declare, clearing the ambition markers for other uses.
- **Ire of the Tycoons** (Lore) — prevents you from declaring or scoring Tycoon (irrelevant to your win), but restricts taxing Loyal cities on Material/Fuel planets you don't control. Reinforces the necessity of actually holding those planets for Edenguard.
- **Galactic Bards** (Guild, in Court deck from Steward Act 2 success or Partisan Act 1 fail) — lets you declare non-Tyrant/Edenguard ambitions cheaply, forcing Rivals to burn markers on those instead. Grab immediately if it appears; deny it if someone else has it.
- **Mining Interest / Shipping Interest** (Guild, Campaign Court) — gain Material or Fuel without needing to tax, freeing you to focus on Edenguard planet control. Prelude actions are exceptional. Use them before Rivals can steal them.
- **Loyal Pilots / Loyal Engineers** (Guild, added from Partisan Act 1 success) — nullify your Fuel/Material Outrage, letting you use those resources and build freely while still triggering Outrage with impunity.
- **Material and Fuel Liaisons** (Guild, added from Advocate Act 1 success) — gain Fuel/Material without needing taxable cities; crucial if *Ire of the Tycoons* is boxing you out of taxing.
- **Tax Collectors** (Guild, added from Steward Act 1 success) — lets you strip resources off an Outlaw's board when taxing their city; good for scooping up hoarded Fuel/Material.
- **Tool Priests** (Base Lore) — city synergy reinforces your Edenguard loop; solid pickup if available.

**Notable matchups:**

- **Steward** — worst matchup. A successful Steward enters Act 3 as First Regent with a strong Empire, blocking Edenguard entirely and using *Dealmakers* to contest Tyrant. Pivot away from Guardian if Steward is in Act 3; if stuck, prioritise stealing First Regent or breaking the Empire.
- **Admiral** — difficult. Admiral wants First Regent and broad planet control, directly contesting Edenguard. You will be forced onto Tyrant; if Rivals also have Tyrant tools, your game is squeezed hard. Consider outlawing and attacking Admiral aggressively in Fuel/Material clusters.
- **Hegemon** — nemesis for Tyrant. Hegemon contests Captives directly and has the firepower to cripple you in battle. Shift to Edenguard as your primary path, but note Edenguard is easier for Rivals to stop too. Win before Chapter 4 — you cannot out-Power a Hegemon in a long game.
- **Survivalist** — competes for Captives naturally (not for Tyrant points, but accumulates them), pressuring your Tyrant lead. Lean toward Edenguard and Imperial/gate control to compensate.
- **Judge** — will force ties on Tyrant to slow you while advancing themselves. Deny them Captives; remember Judge's Captives can be raided away.
- **Magnate** — can scrap Fuel/Material, shrinking the supply you need for Captives and shifting your focus toward Edenguard and First Regent.
- **Overlord** — will incur Fuel/Material Outrage and try to hold those planets, directly blocking both your paths. Wage war early; if they lock Edenguard, Tyrant becomes your sole route and you are in trouble.
- **Peacekeeper** — Peace locks out your ransacking (your main Tyrant-protection tool). Grab a few Weapons in Chapter 1 before Peacekeeper can lock down all five. Also competes for First Regent.

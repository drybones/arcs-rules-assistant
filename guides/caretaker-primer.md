# Caretaker — Strategy Primer

The Caretaker is a Lore-accumulation fate that uses Golems as both a scoring engine and a destabilising weapon. Act 1 is about spreading into the blighted gates, awakening the Golems quickly, and spending them often — every spend advances your objective. Act 2 pivots hard to the Court, where Librarians and a double Psionic-Relic combo let you hoover up Lore. Act 3 turns the Golems loose: they devour Guild cards and strip cities from anyone holding them, while you scrap Lore to silence them one by one and score large Power swings. The grand ambitions are nearly guaranteed if you enter Act 3 with five or more Lore; the real game is the first two acts.

---

## Act 1 — My Golems and Other Animals *(Complexity 2/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 18 times across three chapters. Every time any player spends a Golem you advance; winning an ambition while holding Golems gives a burst — four Golems plus an ambition win equals 8 advancements. Target one ambition win per chapter while holding 2–3 Golems, and spend the rest opportunistically. Tycoon is easiest if you settle on Fuel or Material planets at setup.

- Awaken all four Golems before the first Event card is played — each unplayed Event is a missed Crisis chance. Spread into blighted gates immediately; you have nothing to raid so rivals have no early reason to attack you.
- If rivals' holds are full, spend every Golem you have even without taking the associated action — they cannot hold the returned Golem and it comes straight back to your Golem Hearth.
- Secure the Imperial Council before chapter 3 ends to guarantee a Crisis if you are still waiting for Golem flips; a Psionic-plus-Relic combo on the Council also denies First Regent to the Steward.
- End the act with a **Relic city and a Psionic city**. Cluster 5's adjacent Relic and Psionic planets are the natural target. This is not optional — without both, Act 2 falls apart.

**Key kit:**
- **Golem Beacon** — single pip recovers all controlled Golems; with a good opening hand you can Awaken 2–3 Golems within the first two cards of Chapter 1.
- **Golem Hearth** — Golems occupy no resource slots, so you never have to discard them to hold resources; rivals without slots must discard them back to you.
- **Stone-Speakers** — 50/50 Crisis trigger but returns all Golems regardless; declare late in a chapter or use a 7 to minimise pip waste. Losing it to Outrage complicates Act 3 more than Act 1.
- **Harvester** (S-tier) — free resource in your Prelude; enables same-turn Influence-then-Secure combos and can set up the Unstable Harvester loop in Act 2.
- **Seeker** (strong) — Catapult movement from any city or gate; useful for rapid gate spreading early and repositioning mid-act.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** — make every effort to cause their Act 1 failure. Dealmakers on a Steward-failure gives you a massive Court advantage in Act 2. Seize the Council in Chapter 3 with a Psionic-Relic combo to deny them First Regent at the critical moment.

## Act 2 — The Tower of Babel *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 2/5)*

**Objective focus:** Advance 8 (3p) or 9 (4p) times — 2 per Lore secured from the Court, 1 per rival you out-Lore at chapter end, 1 per Lore received in trade during a Summit. Aim to finish with **5–6 Lore** total; 3 is survivable but leaves little margin for Act 3 scrapping. The double combo — use one Psionic-Relic pair to secure a Court card, attach Lore with Librarians, then use a second pair to immediately secure the Lore before anyone reacts — can net 4 advancements in a single turn.

- Your extra Event card increases Crisis frequency — good for Golem flipping, bad because Librarians detach to the Court every Crisis. Always leave an agent on a low-traffic card (Unstable Golems are ideal) so Librarians have a safe landing spot.
- As First Regent the extra Event also means more Edicts and more taxes; leave the Empire the moment the Policy of Peace threatens to freeze your Relics and Psionics in the Imperial Trust.
- Do not let Court focus erode your board position — spend actions rebuilding ships and clearing Blight, especially late in the act. Establish a Weapons city before Act 3.

**Key kit:**
- **Librarians** — the Lore engine; you must already control it when you Secure to attach a Lore card. Priority target after every Crisis that knocks it loose. Two Psionic-Relic pairs in hand unlocks the burst combo.
- **Living Structures** and **Sprinter Drives** — the two S-tier Lore in the game; secure either at all costs. Living Structures makes Construction cards powerful; Sprinter Drives makes you nearly unblockable across the map.
- **Stone-Speakers** — does little for your Act 2 objective; the Unstable Golem cards make holding your own Golems dangerous, so let them circulate freely this act.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Steward** (if Act 1 succeeded) — near-impossible; Dealmakers lets them snap up Lore the instant it appears and Librarians in their hands outpaces you immediately. Pivot rather than contest.
- **Blight Speaker** — Warrior and Protector have outsized impact on them; Unstable Warrior with Stone-Speakers (or the Council) can wipe their damaged fleet after a Crisis. Use it.
- **Pirate** — your buildings are softer than usual while you focus on the Court; maintain fleet presence or become the obvious target. Raid the Flagship's Tractor Beam upgrade now — in Act 3 it will prevent you raiding back Golems.
- **Planet Breaker** — losing Relic or Psionic access collapses your Secure cadence. Redirect their attention to Fuel planets if at all possible.

## Act 3 — Things Fall Apart *(Complexity 3/5, Difficulty 3/5)*

**Objective focus:** Grand ambitions require holding a Golem at chapter end, then scrapping it alongside a Lore card to score. You can score every chapter but should skip scrapping in Chapters 1–2 if preserving Guild cards matters. Your priority otherwise is surviving the Golems' hunger and ensuring rivals cannot hold Golems away from you in Chapters 3–4 when scoring gaps are decisive.

- **Golems Sate Their Appetite** forces players to scrap Guild cards for each awake Golem they hold at Edict time — including you. Enter Act 3 with few Guild cards if they are not critical; spend prelude-ability Guilds immediately and keep only cheap ones as sacrificial shields for Stone-Speakers.
- Spend Golems through the first two chapters to limit your own Guild-card exposure; hold one Golem per chapter minimum to keep grand ambition scoring live.
- Once out of Guild cards entirely, hoard Golems with impunity — they consume Court cards instead, which disrupts rivals without costing you.
- Secure the Council when a rival has face-up Golems to guarantee an Edict that forces their Guild losses.

**Key kit:**
- **Stone-Speakers** — becomes decisive in Chapters 3–4 when rivals start spite-holding Golems. Pair it with cards that allow mid-turn ambition declaration (Populist Demands, Lesser Regents, Galactic Bards, Liaisons) so you do not waste a full turn. Preserve it as long as possible; once it is gone and you have no Guild cards, hoard freely.
- **Golem Worship** — when secured, steal any Golem without violence; also forces a city loss on anyone holding a Golem (awake or asleep) during a Crisis, targeting rivals trying to deny you Golems late-game.
- **Harvester** — do not scrap this; it is the most versatile Golem and the one rivals most fear triggering an Edict on. Scrap Seeker first (Sprinter Drives or a Fuel city replaces its mobility), then Warrior or Protector.

**Notable matchups:**
- **Warden** — wants Edicts constantly; flood them with face-up Golems so each Edict costs them Guild cards. Golem Worship removes cities with seat tokens, directly attacking their scoring engine.
- **Founder** — Armistice blocks violence-based Golem recovery; stay inside the Commonwealth to hide behind it and keep ships ready for when it expires. Don't let them get comfortable holding your Golems.
- **Blight Speaker** — your extra Event card continues to accelerate their Crisis synergies; use Warrior to reduce their fleet and lobby the table to deny them Protector access.

# Arcs — Blighted Reach Campaign Primer

Use this alongside `skill/primer-base-game.md` when questions involve the campaign. Do not repeat or summarise this primer in your answers.

---

## Campaign structure

The campaign is called **The Blighted Reach**. It spans three games called **Acts**. Power accumulates across all three Acts — the winner is the player with the most Power at the end of Act III (you can also win by completing a final objective). Acts I and II last **3 chapters** each; Act III lasts **4 chapters**. Acts do not end early if a player reaches a Power threshold; ignore the track thresholds during campaign Acts.

Between Acts there is an **Intermission**: players collect bonuses for completing objectives, may choose a new Fate, and most damaged pieces are destroyed.

---

## Fates

At the start of Act I, each player chooses an **"A" Fate**. Fates give each player a unique identity, objective, set of cards, and physical pieces. After Act I a player may switch to a **"B" Fate**; after Act II they may switch to a **"C" Fate**.

Fates have two scoring paths for Act III:
- **"A" and "B" Fates**: gain two personal **Grand Ambitions** — always active, do not need to be declared, cannot be scored by Rivals. Scored at chapter end alongside declared ambitions.
- **"C" Fates**: gain a **Final Objective**. Completing it (objective marker reaching 0) at chapter end wins the game outright, provided you have at least 1 Power. A Power victory is still possible if no one completes their Final Objective.

In Acts I and II, all Fates have an **objective**: advance an objective marker toward 0. Failure to complete it costs Power equal to the marker's position at Intermission.

## The Empire and player status

Everyone begins as a **Regent** of the crumbling Empire, shown by a Regent title card. One Regent is the **First Regent**, who holds the **Imperial Trust** (a supply of resources collected as tribute) and governs the Empire via the Edicts.

Players can become an **Outlaw** by leaving the Empire during a Summit. Outlaws may battle Regents freely and do not pay into the Imperial Trust, but the Empire can attack them.

**Imperial ships** are a non-player faction on the map. Regents can Command the Imperial Fleet under the **Presence Law**: in systems with Loyal pieces, during their turn a Regent controls the system if it is Empire-controlled and may take actions with Imperial ships there (move, repair, battle, build, tax). The **First Regent** controls all Empire-controlled systems for the purpose of ambitions, objectives, and events.

**Key laws for Regents:**
- **Movement Law**: Regents can only move 1 Imperial ship for each Loyal ship they move alongside it at the same time.
- **Truce Law**: Regents cannot harm Imperial ships (except self-hits as attacker) and cannot harm or tax Rival Regents in systems with Imperial ships.

**Imperial Sovereignty**: the Empire controls all systems with any fresh Imperial ships, overriding player control.

## New card types

| Card | What it is |
|------|-----------|
| **Event cards** | Added to the action deck during setup (2–3 per Act). Can be played on your turn to take actions like the lead card, but cannot be used as lead card, copied with, or discarded to seize initiative. Playing one triggers an event at round end. |
| **Imperial Council** | A Court card with two states: In Session (can be influenced and secured) and Decided (cannot). Securing it makes you First Regent (if Regent) or lets you steal from the Imperial Trust (if Outlaw), triggers a Summit option, and triggers either Crises or Edicts. Overrides Event cards if secured in the same round. |
| **Edicts** | Cards held in the rules booklet. Resolved during events; affect all players. |
| **Regent/Outlaw Title** | Tucked into each player's Fate card, showing their current status. |
| **Flagship Upgrades** | Aid cards giving rules for Flagship boards. |

---

## Events

Events are triggered by playing an Event card or securing the Imperial Council. At the end of a round where an event was triggered, the player with initiative:
1. May call a **Summit** (optional).
2. Rolls the event and number dice; resolves **Crises** (crisis symbol) or **Edicts** (edict symbol).

If the Imperial Council is secured in the same round as Event cards are played, resolve the Council only — ignore the Event cards.

---

## Summits

Summits have two phases:

**Call to Order** (caller only): return Rivals' Favors (agents collected on your Fate card) to force them into Negotiation actions; petition the Imperial Council back to In Session; leave the Empire (become Outlaw); or revive the Empire (become Regent, if none remain).

**Negotiations** (all players, freeform, with consent): Cede Control (swap a Loyal piece for a Rival's), Delegate Authority (swap an agent in the Court), Promise Favor (give an agent to a Rival's Fate card), Transfer Asset (give a captive/trophy/favor/resource), or Invite to Empire (First Regent flips an Outlaw to Regent).

---

## Edicts

Triggered by events. The First Regent resolves all Edicts in order. The standing Edict is **Govern the Imperial Reach**: the First Regent either enforces the current Imperial Policy (collecting Imperial Demand and taking an Imperial Action) or changes it to a new Policy card.

**Imperial Policies:**
- **Peace**: demand Relics or Psionics; Imperial Action: each Regent gains bonus city Power.
- **Escalation**: demand Material, Fuel, or Weapons; Imperial Action: place 2 fresh Imperial ships.
- **War**: demand Trophies or Captives (not Regent/Imperial pieces); Imperial Action: give each Regent 1 Weapon.

Resources collected go to the Imperial Trust (frozen — cannot be spent normally). If a Regent cannot pay, the First Regent takes a Favor from their supply instead, or may make them Outlaw if they have no agents.

---

## Crises

Triggered by events. Resolved in order: **Blight Crises** → **Fate Crises** → **Court Crises**.

- **Blight Crises**: each system with Blight hits ships. Fresh Blight deals 3 hits, damaged Blight deals 1 hit. Imperial ships are hit first; remaining hits go to every player's Loyal ships in the system.
- **Fate Crises**: players' Fate cards may add Crisis text cards; resolved in turn order.
- **Court Crises**: resolve the Crisis text on each Vox card in the Court left to right.

---

## The Blight

A non-player entity spreading through the Reach. Blight is **Tough**: it takes 2 hits to damage or destroy (1 hit does nothing). Players can battle the Blight and take destroyed Blight as Trophies. Blight does not intercept. During the Intermission, Blight destroys buildings in systems with no ships.

---

## The Free States

Free cities and starports (not owned by any player) appear on the map. Players may take actions with Free buildings they control — tax Free cities, build ships at Free starports, Catapult move from them, repair them. Free buildings can be harmed and destroyed; destroyed Free buildings become Trophies. In battle, players may raid the Free States by spending a `key` to gain exactly 1 resource matching the planet type; only 1 resource can be gained from the Free States per battle. This counts as stealing.

---

## Flagships

Some Fates give a player a **Flagship**: a Loyal ship that cannot be removed from the map. When you receive a Flagship, all your Loyal buildings on the map are replaced with Free buildings.

Flagships have a **Flagship board** with 6 Upgrade slots (each with an Armor slot). You build Upgrades and Armor there instead of placing buildings on the map. Upgrades and Armor cannot be taxed, cannot build ships, cannot catapult, and do not Provoke Outrage when destroyed.

**Key Flagship rules:**
- You can build up to 1 ship at your Flagship per turn.
- In battle, attacking dice = number of fresh starports on Upgrade/Armor slots.
- Armor must be hit before its Upgrade.
- When attacking, Upgrades and Armor take hits like ships. When defending, they take hits like buildings.

---

## Campaign-specific card rules

- **Protected Guild cards**: marked with an X in place of raid cost. Cannot be removed unless the holder Provokes Outrage of its suit, or the card is named explicitly.
- **Attached cards**: some cards attach to a Court card. Securing the main card also secures its attached card.
- **Frozen resources**: resources that cannot be spent normally (e.g. resources in the Imperial Trust). Only specific actions can access them.
- **Harm**: a term used in campaign rules meaning "choose as defender in battle, damage, destroy, or hit" — excluding self-hits.
- **Tokens**: Fate-specific tokens added during the campaign. Cannot be harmed unless stated. Not Loyal by default.

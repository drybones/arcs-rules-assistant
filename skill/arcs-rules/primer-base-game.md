# Arcs — Base Game Primer

Use this to interpret questions correctly. Do not repeat or summarise this primer in your answers.

---

## Game structure

A game lasts 3–5 **chapters**. Each chapter runs until all players run out of action cards, then Power is scored from ambitions. A player wins when any player reaches the Power threshold at chapter end: 27 Power (4 players), 30 Power (3 players), 33 Power (2 players). If no one reaches the threshold, the game can run to a maximum of 5 chapters.

Each chapter is a series of **rounds**. In a round, every player takes one turn in order, starting with the player holding the **initiative marker**.

---

## Turns and action cards

The initiative player plays a **lead card** face up. Other players then take turns in clockwise order, each playing one card in one of three ways:
- **Surpass**: same suit as the lead card, higher number — take actions equal to the pips on *your* card.
- **Copy**: any card face down — take one action from the *lead card*.
- **Pivot**: different suit, any number — take one action from *your* card.

**Seizing the initiative**: any non-initiative player can seize the initiative by playing a second card face down alongside their turn card (discarding both) *or* by surpassing with a "7" card (4-player only). Only one player can seize per round. After the round ends, initiative passes to the player who surpassed with the highest number, or stays with the seizer if someone seized.

---

## Actions by suit

Each card suit unlocks specific actions via its pips:
- **Administration**: Tax, Repair, Influence
- **Aggression**: Battle, Move, Secure
- **Construction**: Build, Repair
- **Mobilization**: Move, Influence

**Prelude**: after playing your card but before spending any action pips, you may spend resources for free Prelude actions — Material: Build/Repair; Fuel: Move; Weapon: Battle; Relic: Secure; Psionic: any action on the lead card. You can also use "Prelude:" abilities on cards you hold.

---

## Standard actions

- **Tax**: Choose a Loyal city, or a Rival city you control. Gain 1 resource of that city's planet type. If it's a Rival city, also capture 1 of their agents. A city can only be taxed once per turn.
- **Build**: Build a starport or city in an empty building slot in a system with a Loyal piece, *or* build a ship at a Loyal starport (one ship per starport per turn). Pieces built in a system controlled by someone else are placed damaged.
- **Move**: Move any number of Loyal ships from one system to an adjacent system. If moving from a system with a Loyal starport, you may **Catapult** — keep moving the ships through gates as far as you like, dropping ships off along the way, until they enter a gate controlled by anyone else or reach any planet.
- **Repair**: Stand up 1 damaged Loyal ship, or flip 1 damaged Loyal building. Can target a piece anywhere on the map.
- **Influence**: Place 1 agent on any card in the Court.
- **Secure**: Take 1 card from the Court where you have more Loyal agents than each Rival. Return your agents to your supply and capture all Rival agents on it. Draw a new card from the Court deck to refill.
- **Battle**: Choose a system with Loyal ships (attackers) and a Rival defender. Collect up to 1 die per attacking ship — **Assault** (hits defender, may self-hit), **Skirmish** (hits defender safely), or **Raid** (steals resources/cards, risky). Roll and resolve: self-hits, intercept hits from defender's fresh ships, hits on defending ships then buildings, then spend key symbols to raid. Fresh pieces become damaged; damaged pieces are destroyed and taken as Trophies. Destroying a city triggers Outrage (discard matching resources/Guild cards) and Ransack (secure any Court card with the defender's agents).

---

## Ambitions

There are five ambitions. At chapter end, each *declared* ambition is scored — first place wins the higher Power, second place wins the lower.

| Ambition | What scores |
|----------|-------------|
| **Tycoon** | Most Fuel + Material icons (from resources and Guild cards) |
| **Keeper** | Most Relic icons (from resources and Guild cards) |
| **Empath** | Most Psionic icons (from resources and Guild cards) |
| **Warlord** | Most Trophies (Rival pieces destroyed in battle) |
| **Tyrant** | Most Captives (Rival agents captured via Tax and Secure) |

There are only **3 ambition markers**. At most 3 ambitions can be declared in a chapter. Only the initiative player can declare, and only before taking any actions on their turn, by placing the highest available ambition marker on the matching ambition box on the map. Ambitions not declared score nothing. Anyone can fulfil a declared ambition — you don't have to be the one who declared it.

---

## Map and control

The map has 6 clusters, each with 3 planets and 1 gate. Adjacency determines movement. Out-of-play clusters are blocked off in setup; path markers make their neighbouring gates adjacent.

You **control** a system if you have more fresh ships there than *each individual* Rival (not combined). On a tie, no one controls. Controlling a system lets you tax Rival cities there, capture their agents, restrict how their ships move into it, and damage pieces they build there.

---

## Pieces

- **Ships** (15 per player): move around the map, fight in battle. State: fresh → damaged (tipped) → destroyed (removed).
- **Cities** (5 per player): built on planets, taxed for resources, open resource slots and Power bonuses on your player board.
- **Starports** (5 per player): built on planets, used to build ships, enable Catapult moves (chain moves through gates).
- **Agents** (10 per player): placed in the Court via Influence, used to secure Court cards.

**Loyal** = your own pieces and buildings. **Rival** = any other player's.

Fresh/damaged applies to both ships and buildings. Damaged ships and buildings can be repaired. If a damaged piece is hit again it is destroyed.

---

## Resources

Five types: **Material**, **Fuel**, **Weapon**, **Relic**, **Psionic** — matching the five planet types. Held in resource slots on your player board; slots are unlocked by building cities. Resources contribute to Tycoon, Keeper, and Empath ambitions and can be spent in your Prelude for actions (see above). They can also be stolen in battle via Raid dice.

---

## Court and Guild cards

The **Court** is a face-up row of cards along the map edge. You place agents on Court cards via **Influence**; take a card via **Secure** when you have more agents on it than each Rival. Secured cards go into your play area and are active for the rest of the game.

**Guild cards** give persistent abilities — often new or modified actions and Prelude abilities. They have a **Raid Cost** (number in top-left) and can be stolen from you in battle. Some icons on Guild cards count toward Tycoon, Keeper, and Empath ambitions.

**Vox cards** are also in the Court but resolve immediately when secured and are then discarded.

---

## Leaders & Lore (optional mode)

Leaders & Lore is an optional layer, not used in a first game.

- **Leader card**: replaces standard starting placement and resources. Gives each player a unique set of starting pieces, starting resources, and a persistent special power. Leaders are drafted in reverse turn order at setup. A leader card is a player's identity for the game — it is not played, activated, or declared during the game.
- **Lore cards**: also drafted at setup. Give persistent special powers. Unlike Guild cards, lore cards have no guild icon, no Raid Cost, do not contribute to ambitions, and **cannot be stolen**. Lore cards appear in all three sets — the base game, Blighted Reach, and the Leaders & Lore expansion — so when searching for lore cards, check all three card data files.
